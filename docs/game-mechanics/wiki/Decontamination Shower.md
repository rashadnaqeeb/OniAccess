Decontamination Showers are wash stations exclusive to the
Spaced Out!
DLC which use
water
to remove germs from
Duplicants
who walk past it in the (configurable) direction its arrows are facing (similarly to
Sinks
).
Decontamination Showers are the only wash stations in the Spaced Out! DLC that are capable of disinfecting Duplicants wearing an
Oxygen Mask
/
Atmo Suit
.
Despite the current in game description, they do not remove radiation doses.
Usage
A Duplicant using a Decontamination Shower to disinfect themself. Note the
Polluted Water
from the building being discharged through the Mesh Tiles.
Decontamination Showers disinfect Duplicants who walk by it, consuming 100 kg of Water per use, while gradually discharging the used
Water
as
Polluted Water
during use. Unlike Sinks, a Decontamination Shower has no slot for a liquid output pipe; the Polluted Water waste is spilled onto the floor as it is being used. Decontamination Showers will also decontaminate a held item if a duplicant carries one through it, removing up to 1,000,000 germs off of the item.
It takes 15 total seconds for a Duplicant to take a Decontamination Shower. The Polluted Water created by the Decontamination Shower retains the same temperature of the input Water that was used to create it.
Tips
As is the same with all other Buildings that can disinfect Duplicants, the Decontamination Shower cleanses Duplicants of their Germs regardless of whether or not its input Water already contains Germs. This allows the reuse of sieved, germy Water without risk of cross-contamination.
Duplicants using a Decontamination Shower in range of a light source (providing them with the "Well Lit" buff) reduces the use time of the Decontamination Shower from 15 seconds to 12.75.
Decontamination Showers can quickly convert large amounts of Water into Polluted Water at a 1:1 ratio, and the Polluted Water output also retains the same temperature of the  Water input that was used to create it. Therefore, placing multiple Decontamination Showers in high-traffic areas may be a plausible way to quickly create large volumes of Polluted Water for projects that require large amounts of it to sustain (such as mass farms that rely on Polluted Water to sustain constant plant growth).
