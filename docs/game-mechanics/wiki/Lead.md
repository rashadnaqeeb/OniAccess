Usage
As an easily accessible material with the second-lowest THC among refined metals (only
Depleted Uranium
has a lower THC), Lead is economical for relatively low-temperature heat-exchanges made out of either
Radiant Liquid Pipe
or
Metal Tiles
and
Tempshift Plates
.
Lead can be used to build
Power
systems,
Automation
systems, and non-
decor
-sensitive buildings.
Due to its overheat penalty, Lead is not suitable for power generators (with exception of
Steam Turbine
) or
batteries
, but good enough for wires of any type.
Due to its wide melting temperature range and high thermal conductivity,
Molten Lead
can be used as a heat transfer fluid in extremely hot applications, care must be taken, however, as it may vaporize to
Gas Lead
when exposed to volcanic
Magma
.
Exosuit Forge
can produce
Lead Suit
with Lead. 200 kg Lead + 10 kg
Glass
= 1 unit
Lead Suit
.
Lead is the most efficient metal and building material at absorbing
radiation
. This can be easily stopped with metal tiles made out of lead. That makes lead tiles particularly suited for encasing
research reactor
chambers and
nuclear waste
storage facilities, as it will retain most radiation.
Production
Lead is naturally obtainable in abundance in
Oil Biome
.
The Metallic Asteroid Field provides a renewable source of
Molten Lead
, which can then be frozen into Lead.
Small quantities can be produced through
Geothermal Power Plant
as a byproduct.
Lead is the only
Refined Metal
that is present as natural tiles on the asteroid and that does not exist in the form of
Metal Ore
. It is therefore obtained directly by mining it, making it the easiest refined metal to obtain (especially in early game; and given you have access to Oil Biome) as it does not need
wasteful techniques
or
power-hungry and heat-producing machinery
to produce.
Other refined metals that can be found as natural tiles are
Solid Mercury
in
Nectar Biomes
or
Iron

and
