# Natural Tile

This page is currently being worked on, expect frequent and significant changes over the next few days.
The term
Natural tile
refers to blocks formed on map generation of solid elements. Digging such tiles gives 50% of the element back contrary to melting where you get a 100% element conversion.
There are several ways to form natural tiles:
Heating a solid element that converts to solid again, will always form a tile (such as
coal
,
dirt
,
algae
,
slime
).
Freezing a liquid will form a tile if the solid created is more than 80% of the element's default mass.

]
Collapsible elements, like
sand
, form tiles.
Shove Voles
exert natural tiles of the element they have eaten.
There are also other ways to form natural tiles such as:
Deconstructing airlocks a certain way (see
Hiidden Mechanics
)
Deconstructing
Hydroponic Farm
tile while it contains liquid glass.
Depending on biome, natural tiles form different edge around them.
Pips
can plant in natural tiles that are below 80 hardness. They can't plant in
granite
or harder elements.
Hatches
burrow in natural tiles softer than granite.
Creating natural tiles
Shove Voles
eat
Iron
,
Regolith
, Dirt, and then excrete natural tiles accordingly. Tile placement is both tedious and unpredictable, and requires copious amounts of food for even 1 natural tile. This is not recommended.
Heating up piles of
Fertilizer
,
Algae
or
Slime
on the floor above
125 °C
/
257 °F
will turn them into dirt tiles.
Manual Airlock
made of certain materials can be surrounded by 7 tiles, with 3 free tile above. Destroying manual airlock will turn it into tile of respective ore.
Liquid Phosphorus
and
Sulfur
can be delivered via
Pitcher Pump
and
Bottle Emptiers
, and cooled down. They will turn into natural tiles if their mass is enough (strictly above 160 kg and 400 kg respectively).
Molten Glass
can be pumped into a
Hydroponic Farm
that, when deconstructed/emptied, will yield a glass natural tile if its surroundings are cool enough, since bottled liquid will produce a natural tile when frozen. Other high-temperature liquids such as
Magma
and molten
Metal
would work similarly, but Molten Glass is typically easier to obtain, via a
Glass Forge
.
Create a floor with any tile with a pipe above the floor.
Molten Glass
or
Liquid Sulfur
can be pumped through a
Liquid Valve
to create small (e.g.
1 g
) packets of liquid (<
1 kg
) which will not phase change inside the pipe. Once the liquid is in place through the length of pipe, you can snip both ends of the pipe to stop the flow. Then ask your Dupes to empty the pipe. The liquid will come out briefly in a bottle but will quickly freeze and turn into a natural tile. This is arguably the easiest and quickest method in the game to create entire floors of natural tile.
For planting
Sleet Wheat
and other cold-tolerant plants, water can be poured into place and then frozen into natural
Ice
tiles. This is especially useful on
Rime Asteroid
, and
Ceres Asteroid
and its variants, since most of these maps naturally has sufficiently low temperatures.
Cooling down 1473 kg of
Nuclear Waste
below
23.9 °C
/
75.02 °F
will form a tile. Since Nuclear Waste normally only fills a cell up to 1000kg, this requires compressing an additional 473kg into the cell. One way to do this is by diagonally building an
Airflow Tile
to force 2 cells of Nuclear Waste to merge. Nuclear waste tiles emit radiation, making it ideal for
Plant Mutations
.
