Biodiesel Engine
is one of several types of engines you can build on a
rocket
. The Biodiesel Engine requires both
Biodiesel
and an oxidizer (
Oxylite
,
Fertilizer
,
Liquid Oxygen
) for fuel.
The Biodiesel Engine does not store the fuel by itself. Additional
Liquid Fuel Tanks
are required to store the Biodiesel. Oxidizers are stored in the
Solid Oxidizer Tanks
in case of Oxylite and Fertilizer, or
Liquid Oxidizer Tanks
in case of
Liquid Oxygen
.
When a rocket with Biodiesel launches or lands, it exhausts superheated
Carbon Dioxide
at
990 °C
/
1814 °F
in an area below the engine, which cannot be blocked by tiles immediately below the engine. Throughout takeoff, the area below the engine will be heated to a maximum temperature of
2926.9 °C
/
5300.42 °F
at a medium rate, 2.985 times as powerful as a
Steam Engine
.
Compared to a
Petroleum Engine
, it is significantly slower due to its lower engine power. However, it generates more power when in flight (
640 W
vs
480 W
) and can travel 30% further. (
69.2 kg/hex
versus
90 kg/hex
). Interestingly enough, it outputs more power than even the
Hydrogen Engine
when in flight.
In practical terms, the increased range allows a round trip to all hexes on the starmap with only 2 fuel tanks (26 hex range) which is not possible with a Petroleum Engine.
