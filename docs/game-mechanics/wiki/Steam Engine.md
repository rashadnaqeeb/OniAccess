Steam Engine
Utilizes Steam to propel rockets for space exploration. The engine of a rocket must be built first before more rocket modules may be added.
Rockets can be used to send Duplicants into space and retrieve rare resources.

SteamEngine
Steam Engine
is an entry-level
rocket
engine. It must be fueled via pipe with
steam
derived from
water
(either from
geysers
or make your own).  The engine can store up to 900 kg of steam. A lower limit can be set manually by a player.
The content's
heat
does not change, even when built within atmosphere, so there is no risk of steam condensing.
The engine efficiency is 20 km/kg of steam - up to a maximum of 18,000 km (900 kg steam).  This does not take into account weight penalties (1 km lost per 1 kg mass up to 4000 kg, then the penalty increases exponentially).
The steam engine will limit you to the closest destinations until you can build more advanced engines.

Tips
Different from more powerful engines, Steam Engine does not require fuel tank or oxidizer tank.
Steam has to be piped in for fueling. Use
Insulated Gas Pipes
and use the shortest route until pipes made from
Insulation
can be made (late game).  After fueling completion, excess steam should be vented from pipes as even
Ceramic
insulated pipes will eventually crack as the steam loses heat and condenses (even in a vacuum - the pipe itself absorbs heat from the steam).
Alternatively, steam can be transported with normal
Gas Pipes
made of thermally reactive materials like
Mafic Rock
, so the pipes will quickly reach thermal equilibrium with the steam. Once the pipe's temperature is above the effective condensation point of steam (
96.4 °C
/
205.52 °F
), there should be no risk of steam condensing and damaging the pipe assuming the pipe is in a vacuum with no external heat exchange.
Cool Steam Vents
provide adequate amounts of steam. Make a chamber with
Insulated Tiles
, place some heat generating devices to prevent the steam from condensing -
Thermo Regulators
can be used to cool
hydrogen
for other uses whilst keeping the steam hot.
Cool Steam Vents
are far from the surface, so the additional heating for the steam should take this into account.
