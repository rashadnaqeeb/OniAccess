# Germ

Currently there are five types of
germs
in the game:
Food Poisoning
,
Slimelung
,
Floral Scents
,

Radioactive Contaminant

Multiplication
Germs may cover tiles, items, buildings, and duplicants. Different materials may affect germs differently: for example,
Slimelung
can only thrive (multiply) in
Polluted Oxygen
and
Slime
, all other materials will slowly or quickly kill it.
Germs on an item, building or
Duplicant
experience some of the effects of the surrounding gas or liquid. For example, Slime stored in
Chlorine
will quickly become disinfected fully, despite Slime causing Slimelung to multiply.
Each phase of matter has a different upper limit on germs per entity (tile, item etc.). This limit is multiplied by entity's mass. Once that limit is reached, the germs become "Overpopulated" and start dying out at a speed dependent on degree of overpopulation.
Floral Scents don't multiply, but they are constantly generated by
Bristle Berries
and
Buddy Buds
.
Spreading
Fluids (gases and liquids) of the same element continuously mix, redistributing germs of the same type as their masses spread and equalize. Gases and liquids do not exchange germs with each other directly; however,
Polluted Water
can emit
Polluted Oxygen
carrying a portion of its germs.
Germs only diffuse to other elements once a minimum germ count is present, and they spread only to cells with no germs or of the same germ type and of the same phase:
Liquids:
1,000
Gases:
5,100
(except Food Poisoning, which diffuses at
1,000

Solids:

Solid diffusion requires an unusually large number of germs and rarely occurs in normal play. A notable exception is
Slime
generated during world generation, which can spawn with more than
1,000,000
germs and spread to adjacent solid cells.
Only one type of germ can exist in any kind of tile, so if you put a Buddy Bud (which make Floral Scents) near some Polluted Oxygen (particularly from Slimelung infected Slime), they'll carry Floral Scents instead of Slimelung.

Surface Germs on a duplicant do not directly expose the duplicant to Disease, but they may transmit germs to other buildings/items that can infect duplicants with disease. They may be removed from the duplicant by usage of a wash station or plumbed wash station, like a

Buildings that change resources into other resources generally do not destroy germs. For as long as the building outputs something, this output will usually retain all the germs of the inputs. For instance, a

Metal Refinery

Infection
Infection can occur from Inhalation, Digestion, and Contact.
Germ exposure occurs when a duplicant comes into contact with germs exceeding the minimum thresholds (Food Poisoning: >100, Slimelung: >100, Zombie Spores: >1, Floral Scents: >2).
After infection, exposure may advance with each tier applying a different Germ Resistance penalty: Mild Exposure (-0), Medium Exposure (-1.5), and Exposure (-3).

Mild Exposure

A duplicant who eats infected food will absorb all of the germs on the food item, typically transferred there by the harvester, cook, or the duplicant eating with dirty hands. Slimelung will quickly die on solids, but
Food Poisoning

Whenever a duplicant comes in contact with infected gas (only for slimelung, zombie spores, and floral scents) or consumes (food poisoning) infected food, they have a per-cycle chance to contract an illness depending on the germ and their resistance (listed below).
If they fail to fight off the infection, the duplicant gains a
Disease

Treatment
Treatment depends on the disease. Allergic Reaction and Food Poisoning can be completely cured with
Allergy Medication
and
Curative Tablets
respectively, and needs no treatment from a doctor, aside from fabricating said medicine using an Apothecary. Allergy medication will also prevent them from getting allergies again for 1.2 cycles.
Slimelung is cured with
Medical Packs
, which is also produced in the Apothecary but can only be used in the Sick Bay, and both need doctors.
The Zombie Spores can be cured in
Disease Clinics
, though they're expensive to cure as they need
Steel
and
Sun Nymph Eggs
to produce
Serum Vials
.
Reduction
Dupes can be instructed to disinfect buildings (
Tidy
) when the germ numbers reach a limit (10,000 by default). There is no way to explicitly disinfect anything else, save for the
Medicine Buildings
that allow Dupes to wash themselves.
Germs naturally die out when the conditions are not favorable. The conditions differ for each germ.
Items placed in
Chlorine
are fully disinfected in a cycle (all germs except for
Radioactive Contaminant
have a half-life of 10 seconds in Chlorine, effectively eliminating all germs within a cycle). Liquids can be disinfected by storing them in a
Liquid Reservoir
immersed in Chlorine (see
Guide/Disinfect Germs
).
Radiation kills
5 germs/s
per rad/cycle in the affected tile (Zombie Spores), and
12.5 germs/s
per rad/cycle for Food Poisoning and Slimelung. This fixed kill rate makes radiation a reliable disinfection method, and often more practical than
Chlorine
:
A few dozen rads/cycle is often enough to eliminate all germs on a tile; higher levels kill germs faster.
Wheezeworts
and
Shine Bugs
can provide a cheap, low-maintenance radiation source for disinfection.
Uranium Ore
Manual Airlock
,
Mechanized Airlock
, or
Hydroponic Farms
provide an infinite source of low amounts of radiation.
Radiation removes a fixed number of germs per rad (unlike heat or unfavorable surfaces, which kill a percentage).
Low germ counts die off quickly, but very large germ loads in a large mass can outgrow the fixed kill rate.
Tips
Although the tooltips of
Plastic Tiles
and
Plastic Ladders
say that plastic is antiseptic, they have no in-game effect.
