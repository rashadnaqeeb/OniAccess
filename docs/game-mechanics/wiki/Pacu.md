Pacu
is an aquatic
Critter
found in pools in the
Swamp Biome
. They produce
Pacu Fillets
and
Eggs
, and their morphs can provide either fresh
Water
or
decor
bonuses. They can be domesticated.
Behavior
Pacu float around in liquids at the flooding level (over 35% of default mass), and will generally otherwise be in the "flopping" state; while flopping doesn't hurt them, it gives them confined status.
They have a short lifespan of 25 cycles and a high egg mass (4kg). This makes them very useful for food and
Lime
. On death, they drop 1,000 kcal of
Pacu Fillet
, which can be cooked into 1,600 kcal of
Cooked Seafood
at an
Electric Grill
.
Their excrement can be fed to
Pokeshells
for additional lime, although it takes about 22 to feed each.
Unlike most critters, which need to be groomed, Pacus are tamed by feeding from a
Fish Feeder
, and this takes much longer, about 6.66 cycles instead of the usual 2.
A Pacu is crowded if the sum of all aquatic creatures personal space requirement in the pool exceeds the size of the pool. The critter's happiness decreases by this difference, divided by that critter's personal space requirement (rounded up).
A Pacu is cramped if the sum of all aquatic creatures and eggs personal space requirement in the pool exceeds the size of the pool, with a minimum of 1 egg.
The pool size is the number of connected, non-navigable liquid cells. A non-navigable liquid cell is either a liquid cell that exceeds 35% of its default mass, or a liquid cell with another solid/liquid cell on top. Non-navigable cells can drown non-aquatic critters. Liquid inside
Mesh Tiles
and
Pneumatic Doors
also count and are treated as one pool of liquid with the surrounding liquid.
Aquatic critters in the same room but not in the pool will not impact a Pacus Crowdedness, although the Pacus will impact the Crowdedness of the normal critters.
Unlike other critters, they will not be confined when inside an open door.
Despite having a lot of space to itself, the Pacu on the left is crowded because of the Pacus in the right section
The 3 Pacus are happy because the pool size is 6x4, even though physically they are trapped by pneumatic doors in a 1x4 area
Pacus will only swim into tiles of at least 35% default mass, and have trouble swimming into liquids that are not substantial. This can be used to limit Pacu movement for performance reasons or to help them reach their feed. Pacus that are not in tile of substantial liquid begin flopping. Flopping Pacus will look for the nearest body of liquid and attempt to move in that direction. While Flopping, Pacus will fall through
Pneumatic Doors
and open
Manual Airlocks
/
Mechanized Airlocks
.
All Pacus have a mass of 200 kg when hatched and when they reach adulthood. Newly born Pacu have body temperatures which are specific to their variant, resulting in heat creation/deletion.
Variants
The Pacu is a temperature sensitive critter with two additional variants. Provided the fish is placed in a pool of the correct temperature, it produces its variants consistently and quickly. All maintain the same behavior, and large egg mass, making the difference largely a matter of preferred temperature, as Pacu's short lifespan and high masses will greatly stabilize an area's temperature.

Tamed Pacu
A newly-born tame Pacu will live long enough to lay an egg, even when not fed, as long as it is not kept with too many other Pacu (9 in 8 tiles of liquid will work). This allows some limited starvation ranching potential, but the yield is rather low considering the investment in materials to set up systems to automatically store the starving Pacu and remove eggs.
Alternatively, simply cooking a tame, fed Pacu's 13 spare eggs over its 25 cycle lifespan into
Omelette
, at 5600 kcal each due to their large size, provides 2912 kcal per cycle average.
It is possible to sustain a single Pacu-breeder via
Algae Distiller
-
Pufts

Algae

Polluted Water

Polluted Oxygen Vents

Sublimation Stations

As the

Algae

An alternative option to

Wild Pacu
A newly-born wild Pacu will live long enough to lay an egg as long as it is not kept with too many other Pacu (up to 10 in 8 tiles of liquid will work). This allows some limited starvation ranching potential, but the yield is rather low considering the investment in materials to set up systems to automatically store the starving Pacu and remove eggs.
Note that wild Pacu can still become Cramped if they are overcrowded when one lays an egg. This can be avoided by moving the egg into another room.
Tips
Wild, uncrowded Pacu will lay an egg when they are 20 cycles old. The parent can die just before the egg hatches.
An adult, wild Pacu can be tamed in less than 8 cycles and taming from the egg takes under 12 cycles.
Eggs retain the wildness of the parent at the time the egg is laid.
Pacu Fry will only eat once from a Fish Feeder only containing Seeds.

Pacus
Critter Species
The Pacu fish is often interpreted as possessing a vacant stare due to its large and unblinking eyes, yet they are remarkably bright and friendly creatures.
Despite descending from the Pacu, the Gulp Fish is unique enough both in genetics and behavior to be considered its own subspecies.
It is said that the average Pacu intelligence is comparable to that of a dog, and that they are capable of learning and distinguishing from over twenty human faces.
