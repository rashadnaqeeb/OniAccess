Uses
Lura plants have an interesting property of not requiring any fertilisers (except living critters). Notably, the product can be converted to water, enabling remote resource-free water generation.
Beetas can spawn infinitely from
Beeta Hives
. By isolating the hive in vacuum, the need for thermoregulation can be minimised (beetas will cool down the lura farm, necessitating heating). When Beetas are used as feed, lura plants can be grown for little cost. This makes the scaling of lura farms very easy, enabling may large-scale designs.
In this case, 1 Beeta Hive can support roughly 11 domesticated lura plants, or 12 in the best case scenario. 13 should be planted to account for harvesting delay. If autoharvest is used, 17 is recommended.

Lura Plant
Carnivorous Plant
Lura Plants are carnivorous flowers with ribbon-like anthers that detect the presence of airborne critters within trapping range.
The Lura's petals are covered in fine, hollow hairs that immobilize prey and ensure even distribution of digestive enzymes. The only part of a critter that the plant cannot fully digest is the exoskeleton, which irritate its mucous membrane.
Liquefied exoskeletal remains are flushed from the plant as needed.
