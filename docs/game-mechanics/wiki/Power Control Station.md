Power Control Station
is a
building
used to process refined metals into
Microchips
to give
Engie's Tune-Up
modifier to one generator in any Power Plant
room
, which increases electricity production by 50% with no additional resource consumption for 3 or more
cycles
(regardless if the generator is running or not).
Duplicants
need the Electrical Engineering
skill
to operate the Station.
Details
The Power Control Station consumes no power but requires duplicant operation. It uses 5 kg of
Refined Metal
per microchip. Errands will only be queued for the station if at least one eligible generator in a Power Plant lacks the Engie's Tune-Up boost. If no microchips are available, duplicants will use the station to manufacture one; otherwise, duplicants will deliver the microchip to the generator and tinker the generator (applying the boost when finished).
For a duplicant with zero
Machinery
, It takes 160 seconds to manufacture one microchip and 30 seconds to apply one microchip to one generator. Additional levels of Machinery will reduce the duration of these errands, as well as increase the duration of the Engie's Tune-Up boost.

Since the Engie's Tune Up buff lasts for 3 cycles or longer, the cost to boost a single generator continually is at most ≈ 1.67 kg / cycle (≈ 2.78 g / s). However, this does not factor in the time needed for a duplicant to fabricate a microchip, deliver it to the generator, and tinker the generator, so the real cost per generator is likely to be lower. Higher levels of Machinery reduce the time spent on these errands (which increases the average cost), but also increases the duration of the buff (which decreases the cost). For example: assuming zero time spent travelling or supplying, a duplicant with 20 points of Machinery takes 5 seconds to fabricate a microchip and 60 seconds to tinker the generator, and the buff will last 4.5 cycles (2,700 seconds), for an average cost of ≈ 1.09 kg / cycle (≈ 1.81 g / s).
Tips
Power Plant rooms use to require a Power Control Station but now only require one heavy-duty generator and two
Power Buildings
so a planetoid can get by with just one Power Control Station.
Manual Generators
do not count as a heavy-duty generator.
Due to the high value of Refined Metal in the early/midgame, wide-scale microchip production is usually not worth the opportunity cost until you can obtain a renewable supply of metal (e.g.
Meteor Showers
,
Metal Volcanoes
,
regal Bammoth
, or
jawbo
).
However, since the cost is fairly low (at less than 2 kg / cycle per generator), it is feasible to boost a small number of generators in the midgame:
Metal Refineries
produce refined metal in 100 kg batches (enough to boost a single generator for over 50 cycles), and asteroids with
Oil Biomes
can gain access to potentially hundreds of tons of
Lead
easily in the midgame.
Bionic Duplicants
naturally produce a microchip every 150 seconds when Defragmenting (during Bedtime schedule blocks).  This has no cost, other than the normal oxygen, power, and gear oil usage of the bionic duplicant.  As a bionic duplicant by default is scheduled for 150s of rest every 3 days, a single bionic duplicant generally naturally creates enough microchips to keep a least one generator tuned up permanently.
Steel
will not be used to produce microchips, but other valuable materials such as
Iridium
can be. Be cautious about setting the whole refined materials category for use.
Notes
Duplicants will only fabricate more microchips if none are available to use.
Solar Panels
and
Manual Generators
are not eligible for boosting.
