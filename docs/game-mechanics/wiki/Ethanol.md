Ethanol

Usage
Petroleum Generators
can burn Ethanol or Petroleum to generate power:
2 kg/s Ethanol or
Petroleum
= 2 kW Power + 500 g/s
Carbon Dioxide
+ 750 g/s
Polluted Water
Nosh Sprout

Ethanol has decent
Heat Capacity
and a very low freezing point (
-114 °C
/
-173.2 °F
), making it an excellent mid-game (pre-
Super Coolant
) freezer coolant.
It's 12% more power-efficient than
Naphtha
, 40% better than
Petroleum
, 46% better than
Crude Oil
, and (after accounting for the differences between a
Thermo Aquatuner
and
Thermo Regulator
), more than twice as efficient as
Hydrogen
.
It's less efficient than
Polluted Water
, but that leaves you very little room for error between the required
-18 °C
/
-0.4 °F
threshold for
Deep Freeze
and its freezing point,
-20.6 °C
/
-5.08 °F
.
Brine
is slightly more forgiving, but much harder to acquire.
Nectar
generally serves as a superior replacement for Ethanol. While its freezing point of
-82.5 °C
/
-116.5 °F
is a bit higher than Ethanol, it has a dramatically higher boiling point (
160 °C
/
320 °F
), and has two-thirds better heat capacity.  In addition, due to Ethanol's much higher density, Nectar over Ethanol can work well as a low-temperature
vertical liquid lock
.
Ethanol has decent
Heat Capacity
, very low
Heat Conductivity
, and 192 degrees range, making it a decent liquid insulator, although mostly in the lower temperature range.
However, once Ethanol is turned into gas at
80 °C
/
176 °F
, its heat parameters change, reducing its specific heat capacity, placing it about 10% lower than
Hydrogen
and with nearly identical thermal conductivity.  This can allow Ethanol to serve as a mildly inferior replacement for hydrogen as a heat transfer gas, but only for use-cases above
80 °C
/
176 °F
temperatures.
Due to the specific heat capacity difference between the liquid and gas, repeatedly boiling and condensing Ethanol can serve as a heat deletion method
[
1
]
.
Ethanol Gas
is one of a few materials that have a
specific heat change during phase change
.
Its thermal properties make it a good option for maintaining sub-zero temperatures for
Sleet Wheat
farms, with
Radiant Liquid Piping
compensating for their low conductivity. Furthermore, the low thermal conductivity makes mopping up easier, in the case of accidents.
Assuming the wood is acquired from a domestic
Arbor Tree
, the Ethanol Distiller converts 1 kg of Polluted Water into approximately 2.4 kg of Ethanol. A Petroleum Generator converts Ethanol at 37.5% ratio, or 2.4 kg of Ethanol into 0.9 kg of Polluted Water, making this process very nearly water-neutral.
Specifically, per 1 kg of
net
Polluted Water input, 9.33 kg of Ethanol can be burned in a Petroleum Generator, producing 9333.33 kilojoules (or kilowatt-seconds) of power, or equivalently, 4.67 seconds of running a single Petroleum Generator.  This means to run a single Petroleum Generator continuously, a net input of 128.57 kg per cycle of Polluted Water is required.
Per 1 kg of
net
polluted water input, 5.44 kg of
Carbon Dioxide
and 4.89 kg of
Polluted Dirt
are produced as side-effects between the Ethanol Distillery and the Petroleum Generator.  This assumes that the 1.33 kg of
Dirt
required for the Arbor Trees (per 1 kg of net polluted water input) is
Composted
from the Polluted Dirt output of this process.
