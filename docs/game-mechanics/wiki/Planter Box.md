# Planter Box

Planter Box
Grows one
Plant
from a
Seed
.
Domestically grown seeds mature more quickly than wild plants.

Planter Box
is used to grow
plants
from
seeds
that can be found in your world either by digging up or harvesting pre-existing plants, or by digging up a
Buried Object
. Using a Planter Box decreases growth time compared to natural growth, but requires additional care.
Mechanics
Plants that require irrigation can be planted in a Planter Box.
Duplicants
will provide the necessary liquids from a
Pitcher Pump
. Irrigation cannot be provided by a pipe.
For plants that require fertilizer it can be provided by
Duplicants
or automated by
Auto-Sweeper
.
A planter box can hold three cycles of irrigation and fertilizer for the plant it contains. For example, if
Mealwood
is planted the box holds 30 kg of
dirt
. If
Bristle Blossom
is planted it holds 60kg of
water
.
When stored resources fall below what is required for 1/2 of a cycle, the planter box will request additional supplies.
Can't grow
Thimble Reed
nor
Pincha Pepper
.
Critters
that eat plants directly can eat plants planted in a Planter Box.
Sweetle
and
Grubgrub
can apply their plant growth effects to plants in Planter Boxes.
Tips
Farm Tiles
function identically to Planter Boxes. They are an upgrade because they can replace the floor and do not have a
Decor
penalty.
However, Planter Boxes also have advantages over Farm Tiles:
Planter Boxes can be built on top of
Tiles
that increase the
Duplicant
runspeed - whereas Farm Tile do not.
Planter Boxes can be built on top of
Airflow Tiles
that allow gas exchange - whereas Farm Tile block off gas exchange.
Planter Boxes can be built in front of
Tempshift Plates
and
Drywall
- whereas Farm Tiles cannot.
Planter Boxes don't block liquid.
Wheezeworts
become useless when their bottom tile is submerged in even a tiny amount of liquid, which could easily happen when
Steam
is cooled and turns into water. Thus the planter boxes can ensure that the Wheezewort stays active when cooling in steamy environments, without requiring constant duplicant
mopping
.
Stored fertilizer/irrigation exchanges heat on the cell the planter box is in. Since gas thermal conductivity is much less than materials used for constructing
Farm Tiles
, this may result in needing significantly less heating or cooling to keep plants within the correct temperature range.
Harvesting plants do not inherently transfer germs from its fertilizer/irrigation.
