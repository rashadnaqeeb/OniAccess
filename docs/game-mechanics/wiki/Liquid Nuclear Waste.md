Liquid Nuclear Waste
is a highly toxic
liquid
full of
radioactive contaminant
. It emits
radiation
proportional to the mass of the waste. It vaporizes into
Nuclear Fallout
at
526.9 °C
/
980.42 °F
and solidifies into
Solid Nuclear Waste
. Unless pressurized up to above
1,472 kg
before freezing, it will freeze as debris rather than form a solid tile.
Production
Produced by
Research Reactor
.
Formed in a
reactor meltdown
: this is generally a terrible way to get Nuclear Waste.
Radbolt Engines
emit a lot of
Nuclear Fallout
, which can be trapped and cooled into Nuclear Waste.
The specific heat capacity of this liquid is much, much higher than its gas form; this is a lot of heat addition when the liquid condenses. Be prepared with some means to cool off the liquid when it accumulates.
Beetas
drop
1,000 g
upon death. A beeta hive produces one beeta larva per day and beeta lives at most five cycles, so hives are a reliable and fast method of acquiring small volumes of nuclear waste.
Radbolt
projectiles produce
1 g
of Nuclear Fallout per radbolt if they collide with a duplicant, critter, or tile.
Containers containing nuclear waste, such as from mopping a puddle of it, leak out additional nuclear waste, resulting in duplication. Done repeatedly, this can result in exponential growth of the waste.
Usage
Radiation Generation
Nuclear Waste emits radiation that can be absorbed by
Radbolt Generators

Radioactive Contaminants

0.17 rad/kg

Nuclear Waste stored inside a
Liquid Reservoir

Corrosion Damage
Early game, the storage of Nuclear Waste in containers like
Liquid Reservoirs
should be discouraged. When placed into a container under normal circumstances, Liquid Nuclear Waste will be ejected after a cycle, spilling its contents in the environment while causing corrosive damage to the container. For this reason, it is suggested to store it in remote open pools.
The liquid also leaks out of devices that handle it, like
Liquid Pumps
and
Thermo Aquatuners
. Liquid Pumps won't take corrosion damage while leaking.
Preventing Leaks
Leaks and corrosion damage can be prevented by submerging the bottom tiles of the container in
1,000 kg
of any gas or liquid. For example, a Thermo Aquatuner sitting in a pool of more than
1,000 kg
per tile of Nuclear Waste will not leak or take damage.
Duplication
The spontaneous ejection behavior of Nuclear Waste from containers doesn't delete the existing waste, resulting in duplication. As of update 494396, the glitch is still around. The amount duplicated per occurrence is about
39.6 g
to
53 g
. Consequently, in order to prevent an over-accumulation of Nuclear Waste, disposal is required for the regulation of the liquid.
