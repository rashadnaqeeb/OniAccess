Dehydrator
Uses low, even heat to eliminate moisture from eligible Foods and render them shelf-stable.
Dehydrated meals must be processed at the Rehydrator before they can be eaten.
Some of the eliminated liquid inevitably ends up on the floor.

Dehydrator
is a building the allows the creation of dehydrated food packs.
Usage
It takes 6000 kcal of any food prepared with the
Gas Range
and 12 kg of
Plastic
to create 6 packs each of dehydrated food.
The food and plastic must first be loaded into the dehydrator and
Natural Gas
piped in.
The dehydration process is somewhat long at 250 seconds (almost half a cycle). The unloading requires duplicant operation for an additional 50 seconds. When unloading, six dehydrated food packs and 6 kg of water are expelled at the bottom middle cell at
95 °C
/
203 °F
.
Dehydrated food packs never spoil. They need to be restored in the
Rehydrator
before they can be consumed. Each pack provides 1000 kcal.
Food that was
spiced
prior to dehydration will retain the spice upon being rehydrated.
The Dehydrator uses Resupply errands.
