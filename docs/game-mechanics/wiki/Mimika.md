Mimika
Critter?
Mimikas are difficult to categorize. Biologists theorize that the
Mimika Bud
engages in this rare variation of reproductive mimicry to improve seed-dispersal and survive the extinction of key species native to its original habitat.
These charming moth-like organisms feature a microscopic cluster of phytoprotein "brain" cells and are driven by a singular instinct to tend to their host plants.
Due to a monogenic malfunction, however, this plant-tending behavior benefits all of the flora in its area
except
its own.
