Trivia
A fullerene is a molecule formed entirely out of carbon atoms in a closed or partially closed mesh made up of joined rings of 5-7 carbon atoms each.  One of the most famous type of fullerene, as well as the most common naturally-occurring type, is a C
60
(60 carbon atoms) molecule, formed in the shape of a sphere made out of connected hexagons and pentagons resembling a traditional soccer ball.  This type of fullerene, from which the "fullerene" category as a whole gets its name, is called a "buckminsterfullerene", after Buckminster Fuller, an architect and inventor that popularized the usage of the geodesic domes that fullerenes resemble. Due to its resemblance to a soccer ball, C
60
it is also called a "buckyball".
Fullerenes have been extensively studied, and are used or are under study for applications in medical imaging, gene therapy, photovoltaic technology, and nanotechnology.  Buckminsterfullerene, when super-compressed, forms into a substance called a "nanodiamond" or "hyperdiamond", which is the hardest substance currently known to mankind.
