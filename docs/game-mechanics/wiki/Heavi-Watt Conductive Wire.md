# Heavi-Watt Conductive Wire

Heavi-Watt Conductive Wire
Carries more Wattage than regular WIRE without overloading.
Cannot be run through wall and floor tile.
Higher wattage wire is used to avoid power overloads, particularly for strong generators.

Heavi-Watt Conductive Wire
is used for the same purposes as
Heavi-Watt Wire
(to carry large amounts of
power
between generators and transformers) and is considered the superior option in most regards—compared to the latter, it carries 30 kW more before overloading and it has a smaller
Decor
penalty (–20 vs. –25) and Decor effect radius (4 tiles vs. 6 tiles). These benefits come at the cost of requiring
Refined Metal
instead of
Metal Ore
; however, this allows for it to be built from
Gold
, which reduces its Decor penalty by 50% (to –10).
It requires a
Duplicant
with the
Electrical Engineering
skill to build.
Circumventing the Decor Penalty
Heavi-Watt Conductive Wire
cannot occupy the same cell as a
tile
or door, so the
Heavi-Watt Conductive Joint Plate
is usually used in place of a tile to move
power
through a
room's
wall. The large
Decor
penalty can dramatically reduce the
Morale
bonus that your
Duplicants
enjoy, so naively running many segments of these wires along the length of rooms where Duplicants travel or work may spell large amounts of
Stress
for the colony.
Among the ways to handle these penalties, it is common to to build separated "maintenance tunnels" solely to shield Duplicants from the sight of these wires while allowing for generators far away from each other to still connect to a single electrical grid. Depending on the space and resources available, one might instead or additionally use
Metal Tile
flooring and frequent
Metal Block
statues to simply overcome the Decor penalties.
