Superconductive Asteroid
A small location with an abundance of solid Niobium.
Superconductive Asteroids offer rich veins of Niobium to Duplicants that can withstand its molten rivers of magma.

The
Superconductive Asteroid
is characterized by a sea of
Magma
with islands of
Obsidian
on its surface. There are rare materials such as
Niobium
just beneath the surface which can be used to create high-tier
Thermium
. While the asteroid on its own looks rather intimidating, it is possible to reach the
Niobium
and
Niobium volcano
beneath the magmatic sea, but it would require some thoughtful planning before heading to the asteroid.

Strategy
Sending two duplicants with one
Trailblazer Module
each made out of
Steel
or
Tungsten
, then deconstructing the landers will allow the construction of a
Rocket Platform
to allow the recovery of the landed dupes and to plan future excavations later on.
For building tiles and ladders, any material other than
Obsidian
,
Ceramic
,
Steel
,
Tungsten
,
Wolframite
,
Diamond
or
Insulite
(before having access to
Niobium
) would melt under the extreme heat of up to
1600 °C
/
2912 °F
. One can build tiles and ladders using Obsidian which is abundant on this asteroid, or build
Bunker Tiles
due to their tolerance to pressure. One can also pump away the magma with a
Pitcher Pump
as long as it's not directly touching the liquid from its walkable platform, then use a
Bottle Emptier
on a tile placed in space exposure, although this method is best used to make the surface safer to walk on than to dry out the asteroid of all magma.
Rovers
cannot dig through any of the materials on this planetoid or build Rocket Platforms, so they are not worth sending down before materials with high melting points (Obsidian or Ceramic) become available for them. After buildable materials become available, rovers made of Steel or Wolframite can be deployed to build tiles against magma to displace it into space. Note that rovers cannot build any building that requires at least
Advanced Research
, such as Bunker Tiles, but can supply materials to blueprints of these buildings.
Also, thanks to the mechanic that the temperature of a newly constructed tile (or any building) would be set to
45 °C
/
113 °F
if the temperature of its material used is higher than
45 °C
/
113 °F
, rovers can be used to repeatedly construct and deconstruct obsidian tiles to gradually cool the surroundings. Once the surroundings is cooled sufficiently that magma solidifies into
Igneous Rock
, it is preferrable to use igneous rock to construct tiles since it can absorb more heat per tile due to its higher specific heat capacity, but care must be taken to not let the igneous rock melt into magma again.
For large areas of open magma, small amounts
Dirt
or
Coal
, or materials that transition into Dirt upon heating (for example,
Polluted Water
,
Slime
,
Phyto Oil
) can be dropped from the surface, as they will form solid tiles of
Sand
or
Refined Carbon
upon solid-to-solid phase transition from Dirt or Coal, displacing magma into elsewhere, possibly deleted by space exposure.
For food, oxygen, and water, the player will have to bring their own resources, unless the asteroid has Lush Core or Frozen Core as a
World Trait
.
