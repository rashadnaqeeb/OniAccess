Diet
When fed plant matter, each Flox requires 0.6 domestic or 2.4 wild plants. While consumption is the same whether feeding with the fruit or grazing on the plant, having the Floxes graze on the plant directly will require no Duplicant harvesting or downtime.
Food economy
Happy
Flox have a reproduction rate of 1 egg every 6 cycles. As a Flox drops 1600 kcal of meat on death, or 1600 kcal in raw egg if cracked, it produces about 267 kcal per cycle, requiring a bit under 4 per duplicant, since they require 1000 kcal/cycle. It consumes 160 kcal/cycle of pikeapple, so they roughly convert pikeapple to Meat at 167% calorie efficiency. This ratio becomes even better if comparing cooked food like
Pikeapple Skewer
to
Omelettes
. Cooking omelettes makes each flox produce 467 kcal/cycle, 433 if cooking the meat into
Pemmican
, while
Soufflé Pancakes
brings it up to 600 per flox per cycle, although also requiring sleet wheat.
Shearing
When a Flox has the
Just Ate
status, it will will grow antlers at a rate of 16.6% per cycle, allowing a well fed, satisfied, tame flox to be sheared for 360kg of wood once every 6 cycles, or 60 kg/cycle. The duration of Just Ate is directly proportional to how many calories it consumed in that sitting. A well-fed, satisfied Flox will always be under the effects of "Just Ate", while a well-fed glum Flox will be under the effects of "Just Ate" for 1 cycle out of 5. When a baby Flox matures to an adult, its Scale Growth is reset to 50%.
Ranching
Stables should have 12 cells per Flox to prevent overcrowding, so a max-sized stable can house 8 Floxes.
Database
Flox
Domesticable Critter
Evenly distributed throughout the Flox's dense overcoat are countless vibrissae-like hairs that transmit detailed sensory information about its environment, allowing it to detect changes as subtle as the shift in another creature's mood.
Floxes avoid overstimulation by whipping their tails to release the pent-up energy. Because these tactile hairs are so sensitive, they cannot be safely shorn.
Flox antlers, however, are nerveless and cumbersome. Handlers who unburden them of this cranial load are often rewarded with the critter's long, slow blinks of contentment.
