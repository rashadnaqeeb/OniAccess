Conveyor Meter
is a building used to track and control the mass of solid passed on
Conveyor Rails
. It will shut off the rail once the total mass meets the set value.
Limit
A conveyor meter has a limit setting:
the minimum limit is 0 units;
the maximum limit is 500 units;
the minimum, nonzero limit is 0.001 units;
For most materials, 1 unit is equal to 1 kg. For items measured in units (such as eggs, seeds,
Data Banks
, or
Reed Fiber
), 1 unit is equal to 1 item. Additionally, conveyor meters cannot pass less than one unit of those items. If an item measured in units enters a Conveyor Meter with less than one unit remaining, exactly one unit of that item will be sent through, after which the Conveyor Meter reaches its limit.
Tips
By using an automation wire to connect the ports of the Conveyor Meter to each other you can create a 'valve' to limit the throughput of a line. For example set it to 5kg and it will pass through that amount and then reset itself to allow another 5kg chunk through.
Since
Heat Capacity
is per gram of material, it is easier to change the temperature of smaller packets
The conveyor meter will melt if too hot but does not overheat, so can often be made of
Lead
and other common metals in most situations.
Bugs
If a Conveyor Meter has stopped accepting material and its Conveyor Intake is occupied, then resetting the limit will occasionally not count the material on the Conveyor Intake against the limit. This can result in much more material being let through the Conveyor Meter than anticipated.
