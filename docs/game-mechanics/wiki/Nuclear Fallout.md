Nuclear Fallout
is a highly toxic
gas
full of
radioactive contaminants
. It is formed in a
reactor meltdown
or as the exhaust of a rocket with a
Radbolt Engine
. It condenses into
Nuclear Waste
.
Nuclear fallout is heavier than
gaseous copper
but lighter than
gaseous niobium
.
Usage
Freshly emitted nuclear fallout from a
Research Reactor
in a meltdown or a
Radbolt Engine
can be condensed into
Liquid Nuclear Waste
at
63.85 °C
/
146.93 °F
(condensation point
-3 °C
/
-5.4 °F
), to create extremely high radiation levels (up to
100,000 rads/cycle
for
radbolt generation
.
Note that
Radioactive Contaminants
decay on a half-life of
1 cycle
, so new Radioactive Contaminants need to be generated for continuous radbolt generation.
Any radbolt hitting a tile that is not surrounded by other tiles or liquid will form nuclear fallout gas, at the rate of
1 g/radbolt
. Temperature of newly created gas is
4726.9 °C
/
8540.42 °F
. Total mass created isn't high as it can't reach over
500 g
per collision, thus it often results in puddles of liquid nuclear waste forming beneath where the collision took place.
A
Radbolt Engine
emits nuclear fallout as exhaust gas during launch and landing.
