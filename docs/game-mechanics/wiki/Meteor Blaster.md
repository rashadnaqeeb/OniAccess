Meteor Blaster
is a
Rocketry Building
. It aims towards meteors that enter its area and fires
Blastshot
at them to destroy them before impact, therefore preventing buildings exposed to space to suffer from impact-related damage.
Mechanics
The Meteor Blaster's range/work area is a 33x33 tiles square on top of the building, vertically centered on the middle tile of the building and starting on the top tile (5th from bottom to top) of the building, therefore sharing a line of tiles with the building itself. The area is visualized by a bluish square when planning or selecting the building. The Meteor Blaster requires sunlight exposure within its range, similar to a
Telescope
; although its interface claims that reduced sunlight coverage within its operating range leads to inferior performance, this is not necessarily true - the Meteor Blaster will continue to work perfectly as long as at least one column of its working range has sunlight exposure. However, the Meteor Blaster requires
direct
line of sight to meteors in order to detect them and shoot them down; any solid tiles will interfere with their line of sight and prevent them from detecting meteors.
Each shot destroys one meteor and costs one Blastshot. When a meteor is destroyed by a Blastshot, 25% of the meteor’s mass is converted into debris and dropped at the explosion. Note that some meteors have special Blastshot loot. The
Bonbon Meteor
drops a
Bonbon Tree Seed
, and the
Husky Mooteor
and
Gassy Mooteor
drop
4,800 kcal
of
Meat
when destroyed by a Blastshot.
Blastshot can be delivered to the building through
Conveyor Rails
as it has a shipping input.
Intracosmic Blastshot
Meteor Blasters can fire
intracosmic blastshots
at the
Demolior
meteor. It takes 100 intracosmic blastshots to destroy Demolior
Meteor Blasters can target
Meteor Showers
on the
Starmap (Spaced Out)
. Only one blastshot is needed to destroy meteor showers. After the Meteor Shower is destroyed, the Meteor Blaster loses its targeting.
If a Intracosmic Blastshot targets an empty hex, it will blast an intracosmic blastshot once a meteor shower enter its range. This has the benefit of not needing to manually re-target the Meteor Blaster. Ordinarily, empty hexes are not targetable. However, targeting a meteor shower right before it activates on a planetoid will keep the targeting on the hex without clearing the target.
Tips
Meteor Blaster can be easily cooled down using
Conduction Panels
.
Meteor Blasters can destroy
Corium
meteors coming out of
Research Reactors
during
Reactor Meltdowns
.
Excavation generally yields more mass than Blastshots, even after accounting for the 50% mass loss from digging (which is still higher efficiency than Blastshots’ 25% loss). One exception is the
Uranium Meteor
, where Blastshots yield more
Uranium Ore
than digging. This combines with the fact that
Robo-Miners
cannot mine Uranium Ore, making Blastshots a useful option for maximizing Uranium Ore yield and enabling full automation.
