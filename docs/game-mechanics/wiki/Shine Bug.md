Shine Bug
is a
critter
found in the
Sandstone Biome
. They emit light without creating heat, and provide a small
decor
bonus. They can be domesticated, and produce Shine Nymph Eggs when captive. In
Spaced Out
, Shine Bugs all give off
Radiation
, 60 Rads at the tile they occupy, with a radius of 6 tiles (including the central tile.) There is no difference in the amount of radiation given off by the different Shine Bug morphs.
Behavior
Shine Bugs are relatively rare, with two or three spawning in the temperate biome. They are commonly found in places with lots of
Mealwood
,
Bristle Blossoms
,
Bluff Briar
and
Muckroots
. They can drown in water and can be killed by attacking them.
Shine Bugs are quite lonely critters, as only a few exist in the asteroid, so they constantly seek each other, and sometimes mistake duplicant-made lighting as new friends, bobbing up and down in greeting. They are, however, attracted to things emitting light, and are more likely to move towards things such as lamps, light bulbs, or other Shine Bugs. Shine bugs will consume any
Gristle Berry
or
Bristle Berry
that is found lying around. They seem oblivious to the atmosphere in which they're kept, although temperature extremes will prove lethal to them.
Critter Morphs
Shine bugs enjoy the largest array of critter morphs that can be unlocked through
Ranching
, each critter morph requiring a different foodstuff and providing a progressively increased decor benefit. They must be raised successively, with each generation providing a chance to produce the next when provided with the correct foodstuff.
They are as follows:

Abyss Bug
→

The sixth variant, Abyss Bugs are a translucent black hue. They have a very high decor value (+80) with a 7 tile radius, but no longer produce light, which by itself would provide a decor value of +15. They live considerably longer than previous variants, tripling their lifespan to 75 cycles, but also greatly reduce their reproductive rate to 22% per cycle. This means that Abyss Bugs (and Radiant Bugs) will not be able to lay an egg before they die of starvation, if tame but not fed. They eat

→

The seventh and final variant, Radiant Bugs are colorlessly translucent. They have an astoundingly high decor value (+200) with a 8 tile radius, produce light with a 5 tile radius, and keep the extended life and reduced reproduction rates of the Abyss Bug. They eat
Barbeque
and
Diamond

Tips
Resin Production
Shine Bug eggs can be cracked at an
Egg Cracker
to produce
5 kg
of
Resin
. Note that eggs deteriorating in storage due to 0% viability do not drop any Resin. On some asteroid starts, this is the earliest way for a player to acquire
wood building material
which is needed to construct
Communal Tables
,
Hot Tubs
, and
Saunas
.
This is also technically an early source of
Plastic
, although considerable time and effort is needed to produce enough to construct something useful - if a happy Shine Bug lays 13 eggs in its lifetime and 1 is used for replenishing the stable, then it effectively produces about
2.4 kg/cycle
of Resin or
1.4 kg/cycle
of Plastic. A full, 16 shine bug stable produces about
23 kg/cycle
which is enough to construct a
Steam Turbine
in 8.68 cycles. For perspective, a
single
Glossy Drecko
can be sheared for
50 kg/cycle
and produces enough for a Steam Turbine in 4 cycles.
Shine Bug Decor Farming
Shine Bug ranches with
Window Tiles
as ceiling and floor can be placed near areas that would benefit from high Decor, such as Great Hall and Recreational Room, or areas with a large source of Decor penalty (such as
Heavi-Watt Wire
).
To make the Decor bonus from Shine Bugs more consistent, place a
Lamp
or a
Ceiling Light
at one side and a
Critter Feeder
on the other side.
All variants between Sun Bug and Azure Bug have the same Decor bonus, so the excess of these Shine Bugs can be allowed to roam around the base, as long as they cannot enter areas with extreme temperatures or areas that must be kept dark, such as bedrooms and
Dusk Cap
farms.
The Abyss Bug variant doesn't emit light, so it can be worthwhile to set up a dedicated ranch for Abyss Bugs to boost Decor in rooms that must be kept dark.
Radiant Bug Breeding
Initial Breeding
Shine Bugs reproduce quickly (2 eggs every 3 cycles), so just feeding all Shine Bugs with
Phosphorite
allows breeding up to Abyss Bugs in around 50 cycles.
Feeding variants with their respective specialized diet can greatly accelerate this process, but requires more management.
Make sure to remove eggs from the stable to prevent the cramped debuff from slowing down egg production.
It is recommended to keep a separate stable of Abyss Bugs fed off a diet of
Abyssalite
before moving on the Radiant Bug variant, in order to boost Decor in places that must be kept dark.
Feeding Radiant Bugs
Radiant Bugs can eat 1 kg of
Diamond
or 1 kg (4000 kcal) of
Barbeque
per cycle. If both are in short supply,
Shove Voles
can be ranched for large amounts of meat.
Note that
Barbeque
production can occupy an
Electric Grill
for a large amount of time.
To prevent
Barbeque
spoilage, limit the Critter Feeder storage to 8 kg or lower, or maintain Deep Freeze and Sterile Atmosphere environments.
Alternatively, feed Abyss Bugs with
Barbeque
temporarily to increase the Radiant Bug Egg chance, then switch back to
Abyssalite
to save on Barbeque. This involves considerable user intervention.
Distribution and Decor Boost
Excess Radiant Bugs can be distributed to places that would benefit from high Decor, as mentioned above. Radiant Bugs can produce exceptionally high Decor compared to other variants.
When Radiant Bugs are distributed in kitchen areas, they may consume fresh
Barbeque
if it is not stored quickly.
Medicine Herding
Sun Bugs can be bred to produce Sun Bug Eggs, which can then be used to produce
Serum Vial
, which cures
Zombie Spores
.
Shine Bug Reactor Farming
Create a 3*3 box of Window Tiles, sealing one of the sides with a
Liquid Airlock
.
Put a
Conveyor Chute
on the center tile to deliver excess eggs from a breeding ranch. Any variant until Azure Bug can be used for this purpose, to maintain a population without feeding.
Place
Solar Panels
below and to the sides of this box. In Spaced Out, place
Radbolt Generators
around to harvest
Radiation
as
Radbolts
.
Continuously deliver eggs using the Conveyor Chute.
Solar Panels
max out at 50-100 Shine Bugs, while Radiation maxes out at
150,000
Shine Bugs.
Note that the overlapping light of the Shine Bugs will render their light radius pure white. In this case, Decor Overlay (F8) or Room Overlay (F11) can be used to check the number of Shine Bugs inside.
Database
Shine Bugs
Critter Species
The bioluminescence of the Shine Bug's body serves the social purpose of finding and communicating with others of its kind.
It is not uncommon for Shine Bugs to mistakenly approach inanimate sources of light in search of a friend.
It is unwise to touch a Shine Bug's wing blades directly due to the extremely fragile nature of their membranes.
The Shine Bug can be pet anywhere else along its body, although it is advised that care still be taken due to the generally delicate nature of its exoskeleton.
