Tips
As the valuable
Isosap
is an ore byproduct of a phase transition, extra care should be taken to avoid
Flaking
. When a liquid flakes and partially evaporates, the flaked portion converts all of its mass into gas, which can make it look like
Isosap
was deleted. This can be avoided either by ensuring
Sap
cells always remain within the
5 kg
threshold (via smaller pipe packets), or by ensuring enough heat is transferred to the
Sap
so it transitions immediately into Steam and Isosap.
If using small pipe packets, reducing the limit to
1 kg
or less also prevents the phase change entirely, eliminating the risk of pipes bursting.
If ensuring rapid heat transfer, it is advised to place highly conductive buildings as a heating medium, such as metal
Tempshift Plates
,
Conveyor Bridges
,
Conduction Panels
, and
Conductive Wire Bridges
. Element ↔ Element heat transfer has a per-tick maximum temperature change, which does not apply to Element ↔ Building heat transfer.
Alternatively, flaking can be avoided altogether by preventing direct contact between
Sap
and a hot element of significant mass, and instead using background buildings like
Tempshift Plates
to thermally connect to the heat source diagonally.
Trivia
Sap
is one of the few Resources that turn into multiple elements upon hitting their phase transition temperature.
It is also one of the few Resources that change its specific heat capacity upon phase transition. Dramatically from
Sap
's 1.1 (DTU/g)/°C to
Steam
's 4.179 (DTU/g)/°C, and to a lesser degree to
Isosap
's 1.3 (DTU/g)/°C.
