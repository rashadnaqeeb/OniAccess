Polymer Press
is a mid-game machine that transforms
Plastic Monomers
into
Plastic
.
Usage
Polymer Press intakes the Plastic Monomer through the liquid intake pipe, converting it into
Steam
which freely exits the machine,
Carbon Dioxide
which leaves through the gas output, and
Plastic
which is deposited outside of the machine in clumps of 30 kg.
Precautions should be taken when using this machine, as a large amount of
Heat
is created. The
Plastic
produced will be at least
75 °C
/
167 °F
along Steam at least
200 °C
/
392 °F
and CO
2
at least
150 °C
/
302 °F
. Products will be hotter if the inputs are above their minimal temperatures.
The Plastic Monomer used is commonly
Petroleum
, but there are substitutes that don't require
Crude Oil
processing:
Nectar
is produced by
Bonbon Trees
.
Resin
is
processed from
Amber
produced by
Lura Plants
.
produced by cracking
Shine Bug Eggs
.
When continuously provided with Plastic Monomer, the Polymer Press produces 300 kg/cycle of Plastic.
Tips
Certain gases like
Hydrogen
transfer heat better and can be used to help regulate heat.
Submersion in a thin layer (below 150kg per tile) of cooled liquid (
Water
,
Mercury
, etc.) is almost always a better option, though the two can be combined (liquid layer with hydrogen above it)
Alternatively, a
Conduction Panel
can be used to cool the building directly, rather than an intermediate heat transfer gas or liquid.
It is worth considering skipping raw metals and using
Steel
, not only for the vastly increased Overheat Temperature but the higher Specific Heat Capacity and much higher Thermal Conductivity.
Steel, however, requires some fail-safes and
automation
, as otherwise the environment temperature may easily exceed
160 °C
/
320 °F
, causing the output Plastic to irreversibly melt into
Naphtha
.
Otherwise, it is recommended to use
Wolframite
or
Aluminum Ore
, both of which have very high Thermal Conductivity for Raw Metals, or
Gold Amalgam
for the +
50 °C
/
122 °F
Overheat Temperature, depending on the temperature at which the cooling solution is kept.
Cycle Sensors
and
Timer Sensors
can be used to activate/deactivate the Press for a short time during each cycle in order to give it time to cool between uses.
Glossy Dreckos
offer an alternative to obtaining Plastic and can provide enough Plastic for mid-game use. They require more planning and setup and produce Plastic at a much slower rate than Polymer Presses, but create limited waste heat (from their own high body temperature), and consume only dupe labor and
Dirt
(via the
Mealwood
they consume, unless wild-planted Mealwood is used).
