Frozen Forest Asteroid
A frigid location marked by inhospitably low temperatures throughout.
The Frozen Forest Asteroid's low temperatures will make finding water and establishing farms or ranches problematic.

The
Frozen Forest Asteroid
is a small starting asteroid with a cold variant
Forest Biome
start. It additionally generates the frozen variant of the
Rust Biome
, the frozen variant of the
Jungle Biome
, and the
Magma Biome
.
Map Layout
The three main biomes generate as their cold variants. As such,
Dasha Saltvines
,
Mirth Leaves
, and
Dreckos
do not natively generate.
Example Frozen Forest Asteroid Layout
Strategy
The Frozen Forest Asteroid has a number of challenges at the start, with the extreme cold being only the tip of the iceberg. The asteroid is known for its severe lack of water above 0 C°. As such,
Oxyferns
and
Electrolyzers
can be difficult to use as the water reserves in the Forests are expended. While there exists an abundance of
Rust
, the frozen variant of the
Rust Biome
does not generate
Salt
for
Rust Deoxidizers
to consume, leaving the player's source of salt limited to
Dasha Saltvines
. An excellent source of
Water
,
Salt
and heat is the Salt Water Geyser, but it is not guaranteed to generate. Mining to adjacent biomes can freeze the base to an early, unexpected death. Insulation and perhaps actively heating the base may be necessary.
As far as food crops are concerned, options are limited to the
Nosh Sprout
and
Mealwood
. Meat is limited as
Pips
and a single
Hatch
. Being the frozen variant of the Jungles,
Dreckos
do not generate.
Reed Fiber
is locked away in other asteroids, namely the
The Desolands Asteroid
linked via
Teleport
.
The
Frozen Forest Asteroid
is perhaps one of the most challenging starts possible in Oxygen not Included. However, the vast amounts of
Iron Ore
and the teleport to the Desolands offers a bloom in industry once the initial hurdle is met.
If you're seeking something different than an
Ethanol
based Mid/Late game,
Desolands
better supports a Petroleum based one, while
Radioactive Ocean
better supports a Radiation based one.
