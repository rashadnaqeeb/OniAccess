Thermoregulation
Bammoths spawn at
50 °C
/
122 °F
and will continuously produce 1.3 kDTU/s. Bammoth's thick insulation also makes them impossible to cool down in the presence of elements with low
Thermal Conductivity
such as
Oxygen
,
Carbon Dioxide
,
Chlorine
, or
Vacuum
, eventually making them overheat, resulting in -1 happiness.
Furthermore, the
Bammoth Patty
it excretes is based off its body temperature. Care should be taken when keeping bammoths in the same room as
Plume Squash Plant
/
Nosh Sprout
. Both plants' temperature range are below a Bammoth's comfortable range, and
Nosh Sprout
requires
Carbon Dioxide
to grow which is not sufficient for keeping Bammoths cool. Moreover, Bammoth patties, excess bammoths, and grazing bammoths all contribute to hotter stables. Promptly sweeping patties, pruning excess bammoths, and cooling through a
Thermo Aquatuner
is advised to ensure the plants remain the correct temperatures.
Strategies
A gas like oxygen, even when extremely cold, can only remove about 30% of the internal heat generation, though this will increase the time it takes the bammoth to overheat.
Hydrogen
is more difficult to work with but much more effective providing 7x more heat exchange than with oxygen.
A very practical way to cool bammoths is using a thin layer of any liquid, which will provide 25x more heat exchange than with oxygen, for example bammoths may be provided with a "wallowing pit" at one end of their ranch.
Rather than trying to manage temperatures it may be easier to simply use a
Critter Condo
to compensate for the reduced happiness, if the bammoth is allowed to heat up without limit its temperature can exceed their survivable temperature of
100 °C
/
212 °F
, this won't harm the bammoth (only external temperatures are harmful), but as a caveat the bammoth patties will be equally hot.
Feeding bammoths harvested plume squash or nosh beans saves the need to manage the temperature of crops and bammoths in the same room.

Shearing
Bammoths grow furry scales at a rate of 10%/cycle while under the effects of "Just Ate". The duration of "Just Ate" is directly proportional to how many calories it consumed in that sitting. A well-fed, satisfied Bammoth will always be under the effects of "Just Ate", while a well-fed glum bammoth will be under the effects of "Just Ate" for 1 cycle out of 5. Once their scale growth reaches 100%, it can be sheared at a
Shearing Station
to produce 5 units of
Reed Fiber
. Note that Bammoths scale growth starts at 25% instead of 100%. Regal Bammoths behave similarly, with the added restriction that they must eat
Squash Fries
to grow their crest ("Regal Fed").

Database
Bammoth
Domesticable Critter
The Bammoth is one of the oldest species on record, with ancient skeletal remains dating back approximately 10,000 years.
This placid herbivore is known for its unique body language: an angry young Bammoth expresses displeasure by flopping down dramatically in front of its opponent, while older creatures with limited mobility will sit facing away from the source of their annoyance.
Licking the ground in front of a caregiver can be a sign of either deep affection or mineral deficiency.
