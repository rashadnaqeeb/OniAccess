Usage
Duplicants flip compost based on
Farm
errand priority and the speed is affected by the
Strength
attribute.
As the building heats up itself and its contents to
75 °C
/
167 °F
while active, and has a very low decor value, the compost is best placed far away from living areas, preferably in a separated room. Despite the visual effects the pile does not emit any
Polluted Oxygen
by itself; rather, it is the Polluted Dirt that is being converted that is emitting it.
Compost helps getting rid of the Polluted Dirt from the
Outhouses
. The Dirt created can be used for farming or inside the
Fertilizer Synthesizer

Water Sieve

75 °C
/
167 °F

Food Poisoning
to fertilized plants. As it is easy to obtain large quantities of
Plant Husks
and
Oakshell Molts
, marking them for compost is an option to get large amounts of dirt with some manual user input.
Comparison to Pips for Dirt Production
Composts are frequently compared against pips, as they both take
Arbor Trees
and converts it into Dirt.
Flipping takes 20 seconds and produces
60 kg/cycle
. This must be done once every 500 seconds. Work speed increases with
Strength
, with each point improving the speed by 25%.
Grooming a pip takes 12 seconds, and produces
20 kg/cycle
. This must be done once per cycle. Status duration increases with
Husbandry
, with each point improving the duration by 10%.
The labor productivity between the two options are very similar for unskilled duplicants, with composts having a slight edge (
2.5 kg/s
for composts versus
1.667 kg/s
for pips). At level 20, composts improve to
12.5 kg/s
versus
5 kg/s
for grooming.
Composts produces hot
Dirt
, making it unsuitable for most farms without additional cooling.
Pips additionally produce food, either in the form of
Meat
or
Raw Eggs
.
Pips poop Dirt directly from a tree, and doesn't require duplicants to harvest.
Pips produce more dirt per unit of arbor tree. An
Arbor Tree
can feed 12.5 satisfied pips to produce
250 kg/cycle
. Composting the Polluted Dirt from an
Ethanol Distiller
for one arbor tree only produces
111.1 kg/cycle
.
However, processing into an
Ethanol Distiller
to create
Polluted Dirt
also produces
Ethanol

Polluted Water
from ethanol power generation can be used to offset the cost of growing arbor trees, making each unit of arbor tree significantly cheaper.
