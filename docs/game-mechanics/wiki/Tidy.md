# Tidy

Tidy
errands
include
debris
sweeping,
liquid
mopping, disinfection of
germy
equipment, and repair of damaged objects within the
colony
.
Most of these tasks will be performed faster with higher
Strength
attribute
.
Duplicants
with a Tidy
priority
will perform Disinfection and Repair actions automatically, but Sweep and Mop orders must be manually assigned.
Disinfect
Duplicants will clean any object with "Auto-Disinfect" turned on, based on the building's priority level, if it crosses above the specified germ threshold. The threshold can be adjusted in
Germ Overlay
.
Disinfect occurs at
Supply Distances
, and is affected by
Strength
Mop
Mop
is an
errand
used to remove
liquids
from floor tiles and place it in bottles that can be carried by duplicants. Duplicants can mop tiles directly on top of any solid tile as long as there is 150kg or less of liquid present. Otherwise, the error "Too Much Liquid" will appear.
A Duplicant's
Excavation
and
Strength
attributes affect this jobs efficiency, and are trained by it.
Duplicants must be on top of the mopped tile
Tips
Mopping can clean the adjacent tiles (green) and any liquid that spills from nearby (red) but won't fully clean them
You can mop tiles at the bottom, or top of a pool of mixed liquids
The most efficient way to remove liquids (but annoying to set up) is to place 1 Mop marker every 3 tiles (on the same level), so each mop marker has 2 empty spaces between them, because each cleaning removes the liquids from the left and right adjacent tile.
Some liquids can spill over into the "already cleaned" middle tiles while the duplicant is moving tile to tile, so in practice this can be inconsistent.
Mopping underwater is possible. For example, because polluted water is more dense than clean water, it will migrate to the bottom of a tank. As long as there is less than 150 kg present, a duplicant can mop up the polluted water tile. Some clean water from surrounding tiles will also be bottled in this process, but the mop task will terminate once the polluted water is all bottled.
By periodically dropping a different, denser liquid into a large pool of another fluid, it becomes possible to mop several tons of fluid either for use or to clear the volume.  This can be a quick way of transporting large amounts of fluid early game before technology begins displacing manpower.
Care must be taken not to drop more than ~150 kg of liquid, as the denser liquid will then itself become unmoppable.
You can mop on mesh tiles, meaning a thin layer of the topmost liquid (e.g. in case of pollution) can also be mopped up by adding them strategically.
Repair
The
repair
tasks are performed automatically to some
buildings
if they receive damage, it can be toggled for each building individually. This costs 10% of the original building's cost
However deconstructing and rebuilding it refunds all resources
Ladders
can still be used while broken, so don't need urgent repairs
Repair is done at
Construction Distance
Repairs happen in discrete actions, and overall speed is determined by
Construction
(Speed per action) AND
Machinery
(hitpoints restored per action)
In practice Machinery has a bigger effect
This will train
Construction
granting more XP the longer it takes
Sweep
Sweeping
is a job you assign to your
duplicants
. Sweeping an item makes it so that the duplicant puts the item in storage (
Storage Compactor
,
Ration Box
, etc). Swept up liquid bottles will be emptied at the nearest
bottle emptier
which accepts the liquid. Deconstructed buildings and mined out blocks fall to the ground as items, decreasing
decor
until swept up.
The Duplicants
Athletics
attribute affects this jobs efficiency, and is trained by it.
Without Sweep orders loose items will only be picked up by others with the "
Supply
" priority, based on the receiving building's priority.
Sweeping happens at
Supply Distance
