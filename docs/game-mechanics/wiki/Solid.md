Solids
are one of the three main substances in
Oxygen Not Included.
They exist naturally as tiles, and can be
dug
into raw materials. Solids serve a wide variety of purposes, from basic building to advanced machinery. Digging a solid tile will destroy half of its mass, while the other half is transformed into movable material. Solids melts 3°C above their specified melting point. The same, liquids solidify 3 °C under their specified freezing point.
Mechanics
Solid
Natural Tiles
will display a "sweating" animation when they are close to a phase change. This includes a phase change to another solid.
The sweating animation begins 2.5% below the phase change temperature and will end 2.5% above the phase change temperature, measured in Kelvin. So, for example, for
Ice
(melting point
-0.65 °C
/
30.83 °F
), the sweating animation begins at
-6.83 °C
/
19.71 °F
and ends at
6.83 °C
/
44.29 °F
. Elements with higher melting points will show the sweating animation for larger ranges, and elements with lower melting points will show the sweating animation for smaller ranges.
There are no examples of solids having a phase change down.
Default Mass
All solids have a default mass per tile. This is used during world generation.
Freezing a natural tile of
liquid
into a solid will form either
Debris
or a solid natural tile if the tile has strictly greater than 80% of the solid's default mass. This state change will
not
take into account the liquid's default mass.
However,
Debris
which changes state into a solid will always form a
Natural Tile
regardless of the mass of the debris. This includes solid debris as well as bottled liquids.
Max Mass
All solids have a max mass per tile. This generally dictates the maximum mass of a cell when deciding if debris can be merged into it. Examples include:
Liquid bottles which freeze into the same element as the natural tile will merge into the mass of the natural tile below, up to its max mass.
Solid debris which phase-transitions into a solid like
Coal
and
Algae
follow similar rules, and will merge into the mass of the natural tile below, up to its max mass.
Automatic Dispenser
which entombs debris of the same element into a natural tile will instead merge into the mass of the natural tile, up to its max mass. If the new mass would otherwise exceed the max mass of the element, then the debris is instead entombed into the natural tile. However, reloading the game will then merge the entombed items back into the cell without limit.
