Growth
Plume Squash Plants drop 4000kcal of Plume Squash upon harvest.
Domestic vs. Wild
Domestic Plume Squash Plants take 9 cycles to grow, producing Plume Squash at a rate of ~444.44kcal/cycle. Additionally, they must be irrigated with 15kg/cycle (25g/s) of Ethanol.
Wild Plume Squash Plants take 36 cycles to grow, producing Plume Squash at a rate of ~111.11kcal/cycle. They do not require any irrigation.

Database
Plume Squash Plant
Edible Plant
Plume Squashes contain over a dozen types of remarkably stable anthocyanins; twice the number found in any other plant. This high concentration of flavonoids contributes to the tuber's vivid pigmentation and tolerance to low temperatures.
The entire root is safe to eat, including the peel. The upper "plume" can be used to brush wayward bits off one's chin after the meal.
