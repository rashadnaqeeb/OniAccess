Beeta Hives are a moderately radioactive nest made of Beetas. Beeta Hives spawn 1 Beetiny per cycle to grow into
Beetas
. They can be found in the
Uranium Biome.
Beeta Hives are considered stationary creatures that occupy the building layer. Although they are listed underneath Critters in the database, they are neither critters nor plants. They behave most similarly to plants; buildings can't be constructed on top and will die the moment the hive's body temperatures exceeds its livable range. Like plants,
Conduction Panels
do not directly interact with Beeta Hives. They do not count as a plant for the purposes of
pip planting
.
Ecology
Beetas gather
Uranium Ore
for the hive to convert to
Enriched Uranium
. This conversion process is 90% efficient and is a worthwhile endeavor to look into, especially when compared to the
Uranium Centrifuge
's 20% efficiency.  (Any material dug up by duplicants loses half the mass, which would make this 10% for the materials harvested in that situation, but not with space points of interest.)
To create a new Beeta Hive, a Beetiny can be placed in a room without an existing hive and allowed to reach age 2, at which point it will turn into a Beeta Hive slowly starting at nightfall over the course of 3 cycles. Beeta Hives do not require a diet and can survive indefinitely without
Uranium Ore
. Every cycle, a new Beetiny spawns from the hive which creates an infinite, albeit very slow source of
Nuclear Waste
.

The hive doesn't take scalding/frostbite damage when it is in an environment outside of its livable temperatures. However, the hive dies immediately leaving 5 kg of nuclear waste behind if its body temperature is outside of this range.
Radiation
While processing uranium ore, a hive emits pulses of radiation exceeding 1800 rads/cycle that emanate from the opening and disperses 6 tiles away. Combined with the over 1440 rads/cycle of each Beeta, and 480 rads/cycle of each Beetiny. The pulse is brief and does not result in significant rad accumulation, so proactive measures like
Lead Suits
are not necessary to harvest from hives.
Trivia
In real life, reactor grade enriched uranium typically comprises 3-5% U-235 while natural uranium is only about 0.7% U-235. This is consistent with the ratio provided by the uranium centrifuge. While the U-238 in depleted uranium cannot sustain nuclear reactions, that does not mean it does not contain energy. In fact, there are so called breeder reactors that can convert depleted uranium into fissile material which can sustain nuclear reactions. The ability of the beeta hive to convert most of the material into "enriched uranium" (in this case, it should be referred to as fissile material) is similar to that of these breeder reactors.
