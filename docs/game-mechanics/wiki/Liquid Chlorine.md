Liquid Chlorine
is the Liquid state of
Chlorine
element. It is converted from
Chlorine
by cooling it down to
-37.6 °C
/
-35.68 °F
(condensation point - 3 °C).
Occurrence
Due to relatively high condensation point of Chlorine gas, small amounts of Liquid Chlorine can be encountered naturally in cold biomes such as the
Tundra Biome
, where temperatures can reach
-40 °C
/
-40 °F
. Chlorine is notable for its very low thermal capacity and conductivity, and liquid state at low temperature.
Usage
Gas Grass
requires 500 grams of Liquid Chlorine irrigation per cycle.
Production
Liquid Chlorine can be created by cooling down
Chlorine
below its condensation point. This can be done with a
Thermo Aquatuner
and a liquid coolant with a <
-40 °C
/
-40 °F
freezing point (e.g.
Ethanol
).
Wheezewort
,
Thermo Regulator
and the
Anti Entropy Thermo-Nullifier
are also options.
