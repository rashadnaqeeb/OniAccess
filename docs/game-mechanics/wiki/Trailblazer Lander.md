Trailblazer Lander
is the Lander with which
Duplicants
land on a new
Planetoid
.
It is deployed from a
Trailblazer Module
when the
Rocket
is on the Starmap hex adjacent to a Planetoid.
Tips
Since it is deconstructible, the 400kg Refined metal (with which the
Trailblazer Module
was supplied with before the
Rocket
launched) can be recovered. When sending two Trailblazer to a new planet, the combined 800kg Refined Metal can be used to construct a
Rocket Platform
, allowing the Rocket to land.
The
Trailblazer Lander
is a means to transport a
Duplicant
to the surface of an asteroid that has no
Rocket Platform
. A duplicant cannot enter it after landing, and it cannot be used to return to the
Rocket
from which it deployed. Therefore, the player must be careful that a deployed
Duplicant
doesn't suffocate before building a
Rocket Platform
, enabling the
Rocket
to land. This is especially difficult when starting a game on the
Radioactive Ocean Asteroid
, where
Reed Fiber
is not available to make
Atmo Suits
until the player colonizes a distant asteroid. Sometimes you can land on planetoids you don't know.
When a duplicant emerges from a lander many of their statuses are reset as if the duplicant were freshly printed, this includes breath, bladder, stamina, calories, stress, absorbed rad dose and more. This means an unscrupulous player can severely neglect a duplicant who is going to be dropped to the surface, as most of this neglect will be cured by lander-magic and most pampering to "prepare" the duplicant is undone by the reset anyway. Some status effects such as most sicknesses and exposure to germs are cured, others like sore back, popped eardrums and sopping wet are not cured.
It is possible to deconstruct the Lander while it is en route. Doing so will delete the duplicant and crash the game shortly thereafter, apparently the first time the game tries to check the duplicant list. This has been reported to Klei.
