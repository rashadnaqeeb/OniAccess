# Guide/High Temperature Systems

The highest temperature that an "ordinary" colony needs to worry about
producing
- rather than cooling down - is
541.85 °C
/
1007.33 °F
, the boiling point of
Petroleum
into
Sour Gas
. The highest temperature that can be reasonably produced by a machine is
1025 °C
/
1877 °F
, via a
Thermo Aquatuner
made out of
Thermium
. (One could theoretically use a
Thermium
Oil Well
for up to
2900 °C
/
5252 °F
, but the logistics of that are significantly more complicated.) Some
Geysers
can go hotter, but their output is inconsistent and the hottest geysers have products with a relatively low SHC.
This is a guide for people who think that's too cold.
Notable High-Temperature Processes
The greatest reason to build a high temperature system in your colony is for the fun of it - but there actually
are
practical reasons as well.
Plastic
can be converted to
Refined Metal
at 100% efficiency by melting certain buildings such as the
Germ Sensor
.
Sand
can be converted to
Glass
at 100% efficiency, compared to the 25% yield of a
Glass Forge
.
Rust
can be converted to
Iron
at 100% efficiency, compared to the ~53% yield of a
Rust Deoxidizer
and
Metal Refinery
.
Hot Work Safety
(no, not that kind)
A Duplicant in an
Atmo Suit
(or
Lead Suit
or
Jet Suit
) can handle up to
1071.85 °C
/
1961.33 °F
before they start taking Scalding damage. This is barely above the Thermium Aquatuner threshold, and pretty much anything within the scope of this guide is going to be well beyond that point.
Vacuum
is your best friend. Gases shouldn't be let near any hot process without very good reason.
Airflow Tiles
are the ideal container for hot liquids, as they only exchange heat with the tile directly below them and the gas inside them (and there shouldn't be any of the latter). The only way for an Airflow Tile-bordered tank to exchange heat with its contents is through the top layer - make sure a
Hydro Sensor
is two rows below the top to prevent overfilling!
Items on
Conveyor Rails
exchange heat with the tile containing the rail and with the tile immediately
beneath
the rail. Ensure that any hot solids have an extra buffer to avoid leaking heat into sensitive, gas-exposed areas.
Most materials will melt at these temperatures - take care to avoid them.
Diamond
has the highest melting point of any buildable
Solid
, but can only be used for
Window Tiles
and
Tempshift Plates
. Its high Thermal Conductivity makes it your best option for heat transfer systems.
Tungsten
has the highest melting point among
Refined Metal
, making it the best choice for
Automation
and
Power
systems as well as
Radiant Liquid Pipes
. A Tungsten
Metal Tile
is inferior to a Diamond
Window Tile
for heat transfer.
Mechanized Airlocks
can be made of
Wolframite
or
Thermium
. The latter is obviously superior for heat transfer.
Obsidian
can't handle freshly made
Liquid Steel
, so
Insulation
is your only real
Raw Mineral
option.
Notable High-Temperature Materials
Liquid Steel
is an ideal working fluid, with the highest thermal conductivity of any
Liquid
at 80, (superior to most solid
Refined Metal
) and the fifth-highest vaporisation point.
Molten Salt
increases its SHC when it boils into
Salt Gas
, providing a little over a 25% increase in true heat when transferring energy from a source above
1467.85 °C
/
2674.13 °F
to a sink below
1461.85 °C
/
2663.33 °F
.
Liquid Uranium
has the highest SHC among the molten metals with 1.69 (the same as
Crude Oil
) and a boiling point well above any useful process, making it a useful heat storage medium.
Magma
is the best base game alternative with an SHC of 1.
Mercury Gas
has the highest thermal conductivity of any
Gas
at 8.3.
Lead Gas
is the best base-game alternative with 3.5.
Super Coolant Gas
is your best gaseous  storage medium, with an SHC of 8.44 (identical to its Liquid form).
Steam
is the second best.
