Peat Burner
is a generator that uses in order to produce power and has carbon dioxide and polluted water as byproducts.
Use
Like other generators, the output mass temperature is dependent on the Building Temperature rather than the Input Temperature.
One Peat Burner can be fueled by feeding 3
Lumbs
. Recycling the
Polluted Water
from the Peat Burner with a
Water Sieve
will produce 89% of the
Water
needed to produce the
Ovagro Figs
to feed the same 3 Lumbs, requiring an addition of only
15 kg
/cycle of water per Peat Burner (plus
24 kg
/cycle of
Filtration Medium
for the Water Sieve).
Note that
Ovagro Nodes
are only capable of supporting 2 Lumbs each (and only if allowed to grow all 24 vines each can support), so the optimal ratio for such a setup is 2 Peat Burners to 3 Ovagro Nodes to 6 Lumbs, producing 960W of power for a net input of
30 kg
/cycle Water and
48 kg
/cycle Filtration Medium, or 32W per
1 kg
/cycle of water.
By comparison, a basic
Oil Well
->
Oil Refinery
->
Petroleum
and
Natural Gas Generator
setup produces roughly 9.23W per
1 kg
/cycle of water.  An optimized petroleum power setup is net positive on water, but requires substantially more research, setup complexity, and advanced materials.
