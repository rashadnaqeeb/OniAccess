Movement
Basic Movement
Duplicants can walk horizontally, or vertically on ladders. But they need 2 tiles high to be able to fit.
They can jump over a 1-tile gap. They can climb up/down diagonally: (1-up + 1-side / 2-up + 1 side / 1-up + 1-side)
Examples:
The duplicants require the green-backwalls to be free to be able to perform the movement.
Duplicants need a 2 tile gap to walk; They cannot go to the red squares here
Jumping: 2-sideways
Climbing:
Climbing: 1-up 2-sideways
Climbing: 2-up 1-sideways
Duplicants cannot climb 2-up and 2-sideways
S-Digging saves on ladders, but is very slow to traverse
Ladders
Duplicants will treat ladders as normal tiles when on top of them, however they can also hang off a ladder (where the ladder is in their lower tile). While hanging their reach remains the same, but their movement changes
You don't need a ladder on every tile
Dupes prefer not to hang on to a ladder while idling, and will get on top of it, or on another tile if they can.
Ladder movement allows for various tile-movement above, but also to hang off of it
Dupes can hang off a ladder, only needing the 1 tile above it. They can hop to another ladder up to 2 tiles away. They CANNOT hang lower then that
Dupes can grab a ladder that is already on their tile (green), they CANNOT jump up to grab one (red), they won't use a ladder on the ground (blue) since they can just stand, UNLESS its off the edge
Dupes cannot perform their normal ledge-climbing movements while hanging on a ladder
Dupes can climb up off a ladder: to higher tile OR ladder
You only need a ladder every 2nd tile; or less if you use nearby floors. However this is very slow (adjacent/consecutive ladders are faster)
Dupes cannot jump straight down off a ladder, nor "drop down a little" to grab a nearby ladder
Doors
Duplicants act as a tile ABOVE them, but not on any of their inside tiles.
Doors act as a tile above them, but not inside them (you need another tile for that)
Dupes cannot jump up thru a vertical door. So you cannot use them between ladders
Doors can be used sideways to break apart ladder sections
Similar for sideways ladder-hanging movement, only a 1-tile gap door is crossable. (Unless they can walk on top)
Vertically: You need ladders in between, or more space to allow diagonal climbing even if you're using sideways-doors
