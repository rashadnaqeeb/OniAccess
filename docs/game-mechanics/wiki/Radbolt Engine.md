The
Radbolt Engine
is a
rocket engine
that requires
Radbolts
to function. Radbolts must be injected into the radbolt input port via a
Radbolt Generator
.
Radbolt Engines
can travel moderately far - further than
Carbon Dioxide Engines
,
Steam Engines
, and
Sugar Engines
but less than
Petroleum Engines
,
Biodiesel Engines
, and
Hydrogen Engines
.
Radbolt Engines
are limited to a relatively low rocket height in comparison to the other late game engines. Like a few engines such as the
Carbon Dioxide Engine
and the
Steam Engine
, the
Radbolt Engine
does not need any kind of fuel or oxidizer storage modules, since it has its own internal storage. A
Radbolt Engine
can fly up to 20 tiles consecutively without a refuel. It has a storage capacity of 4000 Radbolts and consumes 200 Rabolts per hex traveled.
The temperature of the exhaust from a launch with the
Radbolt Engine
is only
600 °C
/
1112 °F
, which is lower than most rocket engines and below the melting point of most refined metals.  However, all rocket engines apply heat to a 3-wide by 9-high trail immediately behind the base of the engine on launch (extending 7 tiles below the rocket platform), and the maximum temperature matter can be heated in this area is the same for all rocket engines,
2926.9 °C
/
5300.42 °F
, the melting point of
Wolframite
, so anything passing through this area should be constructed of
Tungsten
.  Note that this heating is applied even to insulated tiles, and even if the engine exhaust gas is not permitted into that area, it is direct heating applied to any tiles or constructed objects in that area.
Tips
Due to the fact that the
Radbolt Engine
produces highly contaminated
Nuclear Fallout
on exhaust, this same fallout can be used in combination with
Radbolt Generators
to drastically increase the production of Rads/cycle, as long as the exhaust can be liquefied into
Liquid Nuclear Waste
at
63.85 °C
/
146.93 °F
(condensation point
-3 °C
/
-5.4 °F
), using
Tempshift Plates
and/or
Radiant Pipes
. The high amount of
Radioactive Contaminants
within the Nuclear Waste would allow the Radbolt Generators to produce extremely high amounts of Rads, up to ~2000 Radbolts/cycle the first time the
Radbolt Engine
is launched, and beyond on each consecutive launches and landings. Note that the contaminants die out quickly on their own (with a half-life of 1 cycle), so repeated launches of the
Radbolt Engine
are required if one wishes to maintain high Rads/cycle.
Trivia
Its current flavor text states "
Radbolt Engines
are faster than
Hydrogen Engines
but with a more restrictive height allowance." This is because, despite the fact that the base speed of the Hydrogen engine is 7.9 and the base speed of the
Radbolt Engine
is 6.8, fuel and oxidizer tanks add burden to the rocket which in turn reduce the travel speed of the rocket. The
Radbolt Engine
does not require fuel or oxidizer tanks, unlike the Hydrogen Engine.
However, for practical uses, this may be misleading.  Due to how engine speed is calculated, which is engine power divided by total burden, the
Radbolt Engine
is only faster if the total burden from modules other than the engine, fuel tanks, and oxidizer tanks is less than 10 (if using a small solid oxidizer tank for the Hydrogen Engine) or less than 15 (for a liquid oxidizer tank).  As the smallest command capsule, the
Solo Spacefarer Nosecone
, has a burden of 3, this leaves only 7 or 12 burden for other modules.  This is also only true if the requires other modules would be the same for both rockets.
If your rocket needs power, keep in mind that the Hydrogen Engine provides 600 W, while the
Radbolt Engine
has no power production included.  A solar panel plus battery module combo is 3 burden (and 3 height) on its own, which is enough to make a hydrogen with small solid oxidizer and no solar or battery faster even with a solo spacefarer module and no other modules.   If the hydrogen rocket is using a liquid oxidizer tank instead, adding an additional 7 burden from other modules is enough to make the hydrogen rocket faster.
For the purposes of these comparisons, it is worth noting that a Hydrogen Engine with only a single fuel tank has only 80% the range of a
Radbolt Engine
.  Adding a second fuel tank increases the additional burden allowance to 23 before a Hydrogen Engine is faster.
While the
Radbolt Engine
has a substantially lower maximum rocket height than other engines, the lack of fuel or oxidizer usage offsets much of this.  For example, a
Petroleum Engine
has a maximum height of 35, 15 more than the Radbolt Engine, but in order for it to have the same range of 20 tiles, it would need 2 fuel tanks, plus either 1 liquid oxidizer tank or two solid oxidizer tanks filled with
Oxylite
.  That's an added 12 or 14 module height, negating most of the rocket height advantage.
The compactness of
Radbolt Engine
rockets makes them excellent options when initially colonizing an unexplored planetoids lacking an existing rocket platform, as many planetoids have relatively short space biomes that can make landing very tall rockets rather difficult, and potentially require rather extensive excavation to reach a sufficient depth for placing the rocket platform.  For most planetoids, however, there's sufficient height in the space biome to place the rocket platform and land the rocket without any excavation at all.
Radbolt Engines
are also unique in that they are the only late-game engine with a width of 5, rather than 7.  As all non-engine rocket modules are width 5 or less, this makes rocket silos for
Radbolt rockets
more horizontally compact, as the access ladder for the rocket can be built atop the outermost tile of the
Rocket Platform
, rather than outside of it as required by the other late-game engines.  This also usually negates the need for using
Gantries
or sacrificial ladder segments to access rocket modules.
