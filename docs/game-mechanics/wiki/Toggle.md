Toggling
is an
Errand
type performed by a
Duplicant
when some buildings change state. Whenever a building is designated by the player to be disabled or enabled, the building will not change state until a Duplicant physically travels to the building to
toggle
its state. Duplicants must also perform the change state errand to change the setting on a
Liquid
or
Gas Valve
, or to lock or unlock
Doors
. This means the Duplicant must first travel to near the building, then spend time performing the errand before the building can change state.
However, some buildings can change state instantly, without the need for a Duplicant to perform an errand. In these cases, the change will take place immediately after input from the player choosing to change state. Notably, a
Switch
or
Signal Switch
will change state as soon as the player chooses to toggle the switch, no matter where the switch is located. This can be very convenient to enable or disable powered buildings by placing a switch into the
Wire
circuit, for example to be used as a light switch, to shut off a
Liquid
or
Gas Pump
quickly, or to disable entire circuits to re-route power. A Signal Switch connected to a
Liquid
or
Gas Shutoff
can be used to instantly open or close pipes, e.g. allowing the redirection of gasses in a ventilation system.
Change in building states that require the Toggle errand include:
Enabling or disabling any building.
Changing the flow control threshold of a
Liquid
or
Gas Valve
.
Changing a
Door
of any kind to Open, Auto, or Lock.
Buildings that change state instantly without the need for a Duplicant to perform the toggle errand include:
Switch
Signal Switch
Doors
can have their permissions changed instantly, disallowing Duplicants from passing through the door.
Changing the output settings on a
Liquid
or
Gas Filter
.
Changing logic activation parameters on buildings such as
Smart Battery
and
Gas Reservoir
.
