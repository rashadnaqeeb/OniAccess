Ovagro Node

A single Ovagro Node can support several Ovagro Vines without any increase in its Water requirements.

Ovagro Node
is a plant that produces Ovagro Vines. If unimpeded, the node can grow up to 12 vines on each side. These vines grow
Ovagro Figs
as food.

The
Ovagro Node Seed

Ovagro Node

Ovagro Figs

The seeds are also obtainable through the
Printerceptor

Growth
Upon planting, an Ovagro Node is immediately mature but produces no fruit. Instead, it spawns two Ovagro Vines - one on each side. Each vine requires 3 cycles to reach maturity (12 cycles if wild). Once mature, a vine bears
Ovagro Fig
every 3 cycles (12 cycles if wild) and continues to generate additional adjacent vines until there are 12 vines on each side or there is no more room to expand.
Ovagro Vines can only expand into cells that are:
Unoccupied by other plants, non-background buildings or any
Liquid
.
Supported by a foundation - either a tile or any door (including
Pneumatic Doors
).
Once these requirements are met, a vine can grow even alongside walls or on a ceiling.
Food
Each Ovagro Vine bears fruit every 3 cycles, which can be harvested for 325 kcal of
Ovagro Fig
. This gives the
Ovagro Node
a variable range of food production, ranging from 108 to 2600 kcal/cycle depending on the number of vines that have matured (between 1 and 24 vines, respectively). As the water consumption is fixed, this makes the Ovagro Node the most water-efficient plant for food (
but behind the Exuberant
mutations
of plants like
Bristle Blossom
and
Sleet Wheat
).
Ovagro Fig
can be further cooked into
Mixed Berry Pie
.

Database
Ovagro
Vine Plant
Ovagros are resilient plants with highly efficient nutrient storage and redistribution systems. A healthy node produces many times the amount of energy that it requires. This is used to fuel the growth of exploratory vines that expand onto the surrounding territory.
Vines are entirely reliant on the node for nutrients. Each vine features hooked thorns that protect unripe fruit and act as crampons enabling the vine to use any empty surface as a trellis. They also make vine removal a tedious task.
