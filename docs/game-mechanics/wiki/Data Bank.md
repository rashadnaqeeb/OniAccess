# Data Bank

Data Bank
Raw data that can be processed into research points.
Type
Tech Components
Data Banks
are a special resource that are used to
research
advanced
rocketry
technologies.
Data Banks have a maximum stack size of 50 items per stack.
Production
Data banks are acquired on
Space Missions
by
rockets
with one or more
Research Modules
equipped.
Every space mission destination has five areas of research which award 50 Data Banks each upon completion. One research module can study one area per mission. Once a destination is fully studied each research module will produce only 10 Data Banks per trip.
In the Spaced Out DLC, revealing the starmap with a
Telescope
or
Enclosed Telescope
yields 3 data banks per hex. While finite, this supply is sufficient to complete several Data Analysis Research projects or to select several blueprints from the
Printerceptor
.
All
space POIs
start with 50 initial Data Banks which can be collected with a
Research Module (Spaced Out)
. These do not replenish.
A renewable way to obtain additional data banks in vast quantities is by crafting them at the
Orbital Data Collection Lab
, consuming 5kg of
Plastic
per data bank.
This building requires 60W and the manufacture of a Data Bank takes 33 seconds, accelerated when operated by a Duplicant with a high machinery attribute
They can also be obtained by studying
Geysers
, analyzing
Artifacts
, searching
Crashed Satellites
and inspectable
Points of Interests
.
Data Banks created at the Orbital Data Collection Lab will be made from
Plastic
, while those found by inspecting or analyzing POIs will be made from
Genetic Ooze
In the Bionic Booster DLC, they can be produced by
Data Miners
.
The creation of each data bank consumes 5kg of
Plastic
. The manufacture time is a function of the buildings temperature, taking 18.75 seconds at
-273 °C
/
-459.4 °F
, 100 seconds at
25 °C
/
77 °F
and 166.67 seconds at
52 °C
/
125.6 °F
+. See the
Data Miner
page for efficiency details.
This does not require duplicant labor, and can be done without launching a rocket. The 1kW power requirement makes the building very energy intensive, possibly prohibitively so at lower efficiencies.
At maximum efficiency, the Data Miner is slightly slower than a rocket with 5 Research Modules, sent to the nearest Destination.
The Data Miner is faster than the Orbital Data Collection Lab below
-110 °C
/
-166 °F
, assuming the base 33 second operation time for the Lab. However, a
Duplicant
or
Bionic Duplicant
skilled in
Machinery
will vastly outproduce the Data Miner.
Usage
Data Banks are consumed in the
Virtual Planetarium
at a 1:1 rate to produce
Interstellar Research
points.
In the Spaced Out DLC, the
Virtual Planetarium
consumes Data Banks to generate
Data Analysis Research
points. To complete the entire research tree, you need 1980
Data Banks
. Assuming you can find roughly 100 Data Banks for free by inspecting POIs, you will need to create 1880, at 5kg of plastic per Data Bank, which means you will need 9.4T of Plastic.
In the Bionic Booster DLC, Data Banks are consumed by the
Remote Worker Dock
at a rate of 0.0133 units per second of operation. It must also be primed with 10 data banks before initially coming online.
Data Banks are used by the
Robo-Pilot Capsule
.
In the Spaced-Out DLC, the
Robo-Pilot Module
replaces the capsule, requiring 100 Data Banks in its storage before it will launch. The Module then consumes 2 data banks/hex traveled.
