Liquid Oxygen
is the Liquid state of
Oxygen
.
Usage
Liquid Oxygen can be used as an oxidizer for
Petroleum
,
Biodiesel
, and
Hydrogen Engines
.
Production
Liquid Oxygen is produced by cooling down
Oxygen
to
-185.96 °C
/
-302.73 °F
(liquefaction point - 3 °C). This generally involves use of
Super Coolant
in a
Thermo Aquatuner
, or can be done less efficiently with cold
Hydrogen
gas in
Thermo Regulator
.
Due to the considerable difference between the freezing and vaporization points, it is possible to use
Liquid Oxygen
to cool
Oxygen
with an
Thermo Aquatuner
. Although this is (
much
) less efficient than using
Super Coolant
, it has higher output than using cold Hydrogen gas for the same amount of space used.
However, power consumption is much higher, due to the use of the Aquatuner.
Alternatively, when
Super Coolant
is not available,
Liquid Methane
can be used to pre-cool
Oxygen
relatively efficiently, and then use
Hydrogen
to cool Oxygen beyond the temperature range of Liquid Methane. For hotter temperature ranges,
Ethanol
,
Nectar
and
Polluted Water
are more efficient coolants.
