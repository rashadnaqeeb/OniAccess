Lifecycle
Gnits have no diet requirements and cannot be tamed. Under ideal room conditions, an adult
Gnit
can lay 1 egg. Whenever a
Gnit
stings a Duplicant or critter, its reproduction rate increases to 50% per cycle for 1 cycle, enabling it to produce up to 2 eggs before dying of old age. A Gnit will not target a creature that has already been stung, or is under the effects of
Allergy Medication
.
Once a Gnit egg hatches, it enters its larval stage for five cycles. A Gnit Larva must remain in
liquids of substantial mass
or it will acquire the Suffocation status - Gnit Larva dies if it remains suffocating for 4 continuous cycles.

Database
Gnit
Pest Critter
The Gnit is an insectoid critter that relies on supplementary nutrients and minerals from Duplicants and other critters to mitigate the outsized energy requirements of its reproductive system.
Gnits' antennae double as maxillary palps equipped with finely tuned olfactory receptor neurons that enable them to pinpoint nutrient sources from a great distance.
These same receptors activate the insectoid's flight response upon detecting certain changes in an organism's respiration, such as the sharp intake of breath that precedes a fatal counter-attack.
Trivia
Their lifecycle is heavily inspired after a mosquito's lifecycle: they begins their larval form in water and requires biting an animal to reproduce.
Gnits backwards is "sting".
