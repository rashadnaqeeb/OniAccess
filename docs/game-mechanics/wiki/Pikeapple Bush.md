Pikeapple Bushes

Plants

Phosphorite

Ice Cave Biome

Seed

Pikeapple Bush

Buried Object

Ice Cave Biome

Growth
Pikeapple Bushes drop 800kcal of Pikeapple upon harvest.
Domestic vs. Wild
Domestic Pikeapple Bushes take 3 cycles to grow, producing Pikeapple at a rate of ~266.66kcal/cycle. Additionally, they must be fertilized with 5kg/cycle (~8.33g/s) of Phosphorite.
Wild Pikeapple Bushes take 12 cycles to grow, producing Pikeapple at a rate of ~66.66kcal/cycle. They do not require any fertilization.

Database
Pikeapple Bush
Edible Plant
The Pikeapple Bush produces a nutritious fruit distantly related to those in the Durio genus.
Those who find the Pikeapple pulp's fragrance overwhelming should consume their portion whilst standing near the plant itself; the shrubbery's gentle swaying produces a wafting effect that promotes air circulation.
Closed-toe footwear is recommended, as barefoot contact with the plant's sharp seeds inevitably leads to infection.
