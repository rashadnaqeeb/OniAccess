The telescope and rocketry system has been entirely reworked in the
Spaced Out DLC
. The
Enclosed Telescope
is a building that correspond to the
Telescope
in the base game. They share the same appearance.
Both the Enclosed Telescope and the
Telescope
in Spaced Out! can be used to scan the Starmap. However, the smaller, new, Spaced Out Telescope has a range of 3 hexes, while the Enclosed telescope has a maximum range of 4 hexes, and requires additional research to access.
Additionally, it provides excellent
sunburn
protection (100%), and partial
Radiation
protection (50%).
With some careful design, the Enclosed Telescope can be used inside rockets, just like the smaller Telescope building, and functions as expected, provided that it is up against a wall inside the capsule.
Each hex revealed by an Enclosed Telescope produces 3
Data Banks
. Revealing the initial 4 hex radius of the starting asteroid produces up to 180 Data Banks from 60 hexes. Revealing the entire 11/12 hex-radius starmap produces up to 1188/1404 Data Banks from 396/468 hexes, respectively.
The Enclosed Telescope can also identify the type of incoming
Meteor Shower
. This requires Dupe operation. An errand will be triggered by the Telescope once the meteor shower is within range. Note that the
Space Scanner
will warn of incoming meteor showers, but it will not identify them.
Mechanism
Like the Telescope in SO, the Enclosed Telescope can be functional without entirely exposed to space. Instead it uses following mechanism:
Using the second row from the top count the number of exposed tiles, including 3 tiles to the left and right of the structure (10 tiles total).
Tiles are exposed if they can draw a vertical line to space without hitting any obstruction other than a transparent tile.
If at least one tile is exposed, the Telescope is considered exposed and able to function.
For each tile not exposed, the telescope incurs a 10% efficiency penalty, increasing the time required per discovery.
When operating, the Enclosed Telescope discover tiles on the Starmap clockwise, starting from the left-above tile of where it is currently in. When the ring is completed, it will scan further tiles. At most, the Enclosed Telescope can discover tiles that are 4 tiles away from it.
A normal
Duplicant
needs 300s to discover a new tile.
Tips
As the
Spacefarer Module
is 12-tiles wide, the Enclosed Telescope can work in any place in the module.
As the interior of rocket module is considered a different map in the game, this means the player can use the Enclosed Telescope without digging through the asteroid's surface.
As a
Science Building
, the Enclosed Telescope receives a +10% efficiency bonus when placed inside a
Laboratory
.
Even though Enclosed Telescope's oxygen requirements depend on the operating duplicant, they do not actually breath while inside the telescope, and therefore do not spend exosuit's
Oxygen
supply (If equipped) and cannot suffocate.
Temperature of the incoming oxygen does not affect the operating
duplicant
nor the telescope's temperature in any way.
While Enclosed Telescope requires
Oxygen
gas to place a research
errand
, it will continue working until the duplicant leaves even if oxygen supply has cut off and its internal storage is empty (If other requirements are met).
This means that it is possible to run Enclosed Telescope on very low oxygen supply, although it may be considered an exploit.
