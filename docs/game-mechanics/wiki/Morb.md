Morbs
are non-hostile, land-based
critters
that can be found naturally in the
Caustic Biome
. They passively excrete
Polluted Oxygen
and consume nothing. While they cannot be tamed, they can be captured with a
Critter Trap
and delivered to a
Critter Drop-Off
.
Spawning
If an
Outhouse
is filled, left unemptied, and not disabled, a Morb will spawn near it after
2 cycles
.
An
Outhouse
will only spawn a Morb ONCE after
2 cycles
, but more can be spawned again by resetting the Outhouse state. This is done by reloading the game or simply by disabling/enabling the Outhouse (whether it's from the duplicant's toggling action or by having the Outhouse built on top of a
Mechanized Airlock
which cycles open/close). It makes refilling the Outhouse unnecessary to get more spawns.
Crashed Satellites
have a chance to spawn three Morbs when rummaged.
Outhouses made unavailable due to gunk from
Bionic Duplicants
will not spawn Morbs.
Behavior
Morbs are not hostile and will try to flee when attacked. They will wander in one direction until they cannot travel further, then turn and travel back. They have the ability to jump walls that are 2 tiles high or across a one tile gap. Like other critters, they may be trapped permanently inside of doors and
Mesh Tiles
.
Morbs, like
Pufts
, only achieve their maximum production rate if their mobility is limited and if never over-pressured. They will otherwise spend much of their time traveling or idling instead of producing.
Mechanics
Periodically, they will emit a cloud with
200 g
of
Polluted Oxygen
infected with 200 germs of
Slimelung
if their current tile meets certain conditions:
Not in
Vacuum
or occupied by
Liquids
.
Atmospheric pressure below
1,000 g
.
Arrived at this tile on its last movement.
If the second tile above them is not blocked, Morbs will show an "emission" animation at this point. It is not required.
Morbs produce
Slimelung
germs.
Morbs' exact rates of production are difficult to specify precisely:
Morb emissions happen 0-3 times every time the Morb ends a Movement, and are therefore highly random and dependent on many specific circumstances.
A single Morb with 7 to 9 tiles of running space in a closed chamber of
100 g
of polluted oxygen was tested to producing an average of 60 emissions, or
12 kg/cycle
, at fast game speed.
The Morb rate of
Polluted Oxygen
emissions is slightly affected by in-game speed and lag, this is likely due to a reduced number of critter movement checks on lower frame rates. A slow game speed may make the Morbs generate
1 g/s
to
2 g/s
extra Polluted Oxygen.
At least 2 tiles of running space is needed for any production at all, Morbs will not produce in a 1 tile restricted space.
Morbs cannot be overcrowded or unhappy, so you can put a large number of them in a small room and it will not additionally impact their production rate.
Tips
Since only one type of germ can exist in any kind of tile, putting
Buddy Buds
next to the Morbs will effectively prevent their Slimelung production, since the Polluted Oxygen will carry Floral Scents instead.
An
Atmo Sensor
should be paired with anything harvesting the emission clouds (
Gas Pumps
,
Deodorizers
, ...), to both protect the Morbs from
Vacuum
penalties, and allow the Buddy Buds to work (they must have at least
150 g
of atmospheric pressure).
Morbs output Polluted Oxygen from nothing, and thus are resource-positive by themselves, enabling
usage of Polluted Oxygen
.
Pufts
stables can be fed solely with Morbs to get
Slime
for free, and thus
Algae
(from
Algae Distiller
),
Coal
(from
Sage Hatches
) or even
Dirt
(from melting). Under optimal settings, 4.2 Morbs are needed for each Puft, and 2.5 for each Puft Prince. A full Puft ranch with 10 Pufts + 1 Prince Puft will need at least 45 Morbs to be sustained.
A
Deodorizer
room to mass-produce
Clay
and
Oxygen
can be set up above the Morbs. To properly separate the Polluted Oxygen and Oxygen atmospheres, the Deodorizers must be placed on
Mesh Tiles
, themselves placed on
Airflow Tiles
, with Water inside the Mesh Tiles. The Deodorizers have the range to reach gases inside the Airflow Tiles, and will output Oxygen at their level, above the liquid. Careful, however, as the Deodorizers don't overpressurize, unlimited Oxygen pressure can be reached, with all its drawbacks (e.g. causing the "Popped Eardrums" effect on duplicants). At least 5 Morbs are needed per Deodorizer to have 100% uptime.
An optimal Morb farming setup would be an infinitely long completely flat running path to allow maximum movement opportunities and the quickest completion of them, but any flat space 7 tiles wide and up would be practically identical in terms of efficiency.
Spawning a lot of Morbs will make their Polluted Oxygen production bottlenecked by the atmospheric pressure, effectively mitigating the seemingly randomness of their emission rate (due to RNG, in-game speed, and lag).
To keep spawning Morbs from the Outhouses without paying attention to the cycle count, you can reload the game every time a new Printable is available from the
Printing Pod
.
Gases heavier than
Polluted Oxygen
, such as
Carbon Dioxide
, will displace the emission clouds, allowing you to maintain a steady atmospheric pressure for your Morbs even in rooms above
1,000 g
.
Morbs provide an infinitely renewable source of oxygen for duplicant survival on planetoids lacking water or oxygen geysers.
Database
Morb
Pest Critter
The Morb is a versatile scavenger, capable of breaking down and consuming dead matter from most plant and animal species.
It poses a severe disease risk to humans due to the thick slime it excretes to surround its inner cartilage structures.
A single teaspoon of Morb slime can contain up to a quadrillion bacteria that work to deter would-be predators and liquefy its food.
Petting a Morb is not recommended.
