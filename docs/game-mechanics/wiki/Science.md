The
Science
attribute increases the speed of all attribute gains which are gained by doing according tasks. This means a Duplicant with a Science Skill of 10 (+100% Attribute leveling) will gain twice the Athletics and Machinery experience compared to a Duplicant with a Science skill of 0 if both are left operating a
Manual Generator
.
Training
As Science affects attribute leveling, it would be beneficial in the long-run to improve Science first.
Research
errands
such as at a
Research Station
or at a
Geyser
are accessible and productive tasks early in the game.
After all available research is finished, science can easily be trained by using an
Emulsifier
to produce
Salt Water
, and then a
Desalinator
to turn the salt water back into
Water
.
Analyzing plant mutations at the
Botanical Analyzer
, and
geotuning
also provides a modest amount of renewable experience.
Tips
It is usually recommended to have a single dedicated Duplicant with an interest in Science in order to eventually cap the Attribute.
Inexperienced players who struggle to keep a high Morale may dedicate two Duplicants to Research (one for Astronomy, one for Orbital Research), as the full Research Skill Tree demands 6 Morale (or 11 if it is not an Interest)
If your dedicated Researcher hits the Science cap of 20 (without Traits or Buffs) and you aim to MinMax, you may use other Duplicants to continue Research and Analysis and train their Science Attribute, effectively speeding up all other Attribute gains.
Duplicants meant to do another tasks but are paired with Science as a second or third Interest are worth to considering, as their basic Science Attribute will speed up their general Attribute gains. Furthermore, they can learn the Advanced Research Skill for 0 Morale cost to further increase the Attribute by +2 (+20% additional Attribute Leveling).
This means that any positive Trait of Buff that increases Science such as Quick Learner or Early Bird pair well with hard to level tasks such as Husbandry.

Misconception
Prior versions of the game had its tooltip for Science wrongly state "how quickly a Duplicant conducts Research and gains Skill Points", which lead to the common misconception that it improves skill level experience rather than attribute level experience.
