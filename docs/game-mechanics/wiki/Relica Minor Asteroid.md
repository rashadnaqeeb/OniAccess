Relica Minor Asteroid
A smaller endangered starting world with abundant food resources.
Relica's biodiversity makes reliable food production more attainable for new colonies. Construction materials and fuels are also ample. Surface defence must be prioritized to minimize damage from an incoming impactor asteroid.

Strategy
The signature Spaced Out starting map of the Prehistoric Planet expansion, starting Relica Minor shares similarities with the Terra asteroid. A key distinction however is the area immediately surrounding the starting zone is hot, ranging anywhere from 29 degrees Celsius to as high as 37, depending on world seed. Rolling a seed with the large glaciers trait may provide a longer cushion of comfort time before active base heating is required.
The flora and fauna around the starting biome all flourish in the higher temperatures, but plants and animals found in the abyssalite-trapped cold biomes require the drastically colder temperatures (roughly -25 degrees) of those areas. This can present problems with ranching both critter and farming plant types close together, as allowing both the hot and cold biomes to equalize in temperature will result in neither type of farming/ranching surviving for extended periods of time.
A unique variant of this asteroid is the impending impact by a sort of doomsday meteor, called Demolior. By default, this meteor strikes the asteroid at cycle 200, which will completely destroy a significant portion of the top end of the map (around 20%). More information on this event can be found on the associated wiki page.
While the heat and imminent meteor impact are mid- to late-game concerns, the initial struggle for a new colony will be establishing a food source and renewable supply of water. The plants on this asteroid require pollination from the domestic Mimika critter, which take 5 cycles to hatch after being planted like a seed. These critters are airborne and can travel nearly anywhere within the base, which will require careful farm planning to ensure they do not fly away from the farming area and leave your plant growth halted.
Water is limited to a few pools around the starting biome, polluted water in the adjacent swampy biomes, and geysers yielding salt water and brine. These can be of the cool or hot variety.
Ranching for food may not be the best use of resources in the early game, aside from Mimikas, due to very long creature lifespans and incubation times for their eggs. Ranching in the cold biomes can be tricky, as the new plants and critters have some unique mechanics that encourage specific ranch setups to be sustainable.
As far as oxygen, most asteroids start with enough algae to support a single-digit number of duplicants well enough until a SPOM can be constructed. However, keeping the SPOM and oxygen output cool will vary depending on the overall starting temperature of the world seed, and may require pre-cooling the incoming water supply or the oxygen output ducting in a nearby cold biome.
