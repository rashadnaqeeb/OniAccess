Sleet Wheat
is a cold dwelling
plant
found in the
Frozen Biome
; it produces
Sleet Wheat Grain
. Sleet Wheat Grain is both a food item and the seed for planting more Sleet Wheat. Sleet Wheat is very slow to mature, at 18 cycles, and combined with the temperature requirements, the colony needs to have a well established infrastructure before it can be effectively farmed.
Sleet Wheat Grain is needed to make
Frost Buns
(and thus
Frost Burgers
),
Pepper Bread
,
Soufflé Pancakes
, and
Berry Sludge
.
In all recipes that require it, Sleet Wheat Grain can be substituted by an equal amount of
Megafrond Grain
.
In most recipes, each Sleet Wheat grain is worth
400 kcal
when prepared.
The plant is notable for its unusual seed mechanics: Sleet Wheat Grain is both the product and the seed.

It produces 18 Sleet Wheat Grains per harvest which can be processed into
Frost Bun
and
Soufflé Pancakes
at an
Electric Grill
,
Pepper Bread
at a
Gas Range
, or
Berry Sludge
at a
Microbe Musher
.
Frost Bun
provides 1200 kcals and requires 3 Sleet Wheat Grains.
(1200 kcals / 3 Sleet Wheat Grains) * (18 Sleet Wheat Grains / 18 cycles) / 1 plant = 400 kcals / cycle / plant
A Duplicant requires ~1000 kcals per cycle. So you need 1000/400 = 2.5 plants per dupe.
Soufflé Pancakes
provide 3600 kcals and requires 2 Sleet Wheat Grains and 1600 kcal of
Raw Egg
. The Raw Egg is given the same value as an
Omelette
in this calculation, of 2800 kcal. That, plus the standard amount of calories in 2 Sleet Wheat Grains (400 per grain x 2 grains = 800), gives us 3600.
(800 kcals / 2 Sleet Wheat Grains) * (18 Sleet Wheat Grains / 18 Cycles) / 1 plant = 400 kcals / cycle / plant
With 1 plant at 400 kcals, you need 0.56 plants per dupe.
You would also need 444 kcal per dupe of Raw Egg.
Berry Sludge
provides 4000 kcals and requires 5 Sleet Wheat Grains. It improves food quality to Good(+3) with additional requirement of
Bristle Blossom
to be planted.  Since 1600kcal (1 berry) of Bristle Berry is required, it's net caloric gain is 2400kcal
(2400 kcals / 5 Sleet Wheat Grains) * (18 Sleet Wheat Grains / 18 cycles) / 1 plant = 480 kcals / cycle / plant
1 Bristle Berry / 6 cycles * 6 plants and 18 Sleet Wheat Grains / 18 cycles * 5 plants allows for the production of 18 berry sludge per 18 cycles.
So you need 1000 (kcals / dupe) / 800 (Berry Sludge kcal / 5 Sleet Wheat plants) = 1.25 plants per dupe.  Since Bristle Berry is also necessary at a 6:5 ratio, you will also need 1.25*1.2 = 1.5 Bristle Berry plants per dupe. It is worth mentioning that this entire process is fairly power intensive.
Pepper Bread
provides 4000 kcals and requires 10 Sleet Wheat Grains. It improves food quality to Superb(+5) with additional requirement of
Pincha Pepper
to be planted.
(4000 kcals / 10 Sleet Wheat Grains) * (18 Sleet Wheat Grains / 18 cycles) / 1 plant = 400 kcals / cycle / plant
So you need 1000/400 = 2.5 plants per dupe.
Mixed Berry Pie
provides 4200 kcals and requires 3 Sleet Wheat Grains, 1000 kcals of
Grubfruit
and 2000 kcals of
Gristle Berry
. It has food quality of Superb (+5) and spoil time of 4 cycles. Overall caloric gain from Grubfruit and Gristle Berry is 1200 kcals and 400 kcals per Grain, which is the same as Frost Bun.
(1200 kcals / 3 Sleet Wheat Grains) * (18 Sleet Wheat Grains / 18 cycles) / 1 plant = 400 kcals / cycle / Sleet Wheat plant
A standard Duplicant requires ~1000 kcals per cycle. So you need 1000/400 = 2.5 Sleet Wheat plants per dupe.
Frost Burger
provides 6000 kcals and requires 1200 kcals of Frost Bun. It improves food quality to Ambrosial(+6) with additional requirement of
Waterweed
to be planted.
(6000 kcals / 3 Sleet Wheat Grains) * (18 Sleet Wheat Grains / 18 cycles) / 1 plant = 2000 kcals / cycle / plant
So you need 1000/2000 = 0.5 plants per dupe.

Spoilage
Sleet Wheat Grain will spoil in Oxygen or Polluted Oxygen. Although Sleet Wheat grows just fine in Oxygen or Polluted Oxygen, when it matures and drops its Sleet Wheat Grain,  it will start to spoil if not picked up. Therefore, in the early game, it is advised to ensure that any wild sleet wheat is covered in carbon dioxide to avoid spoilage.

Tips
Since Berry Sludge is extremely useful  for rocket trips (it doesn't spoil), and Sleet Wheat is difficult to farm in the early game, it is strongly advised to keep and harvest any wild sleet wheat found on the starting asteroid.
For beginners, it is recommended to plant Sleet Wheat in
Planter Boxes
or
Farm Tiles
rather than
Hydroponic Farms
as the pumped
water
can turn into
ice
and damage pipes if below
-0.6 °C
/
30.92 °F
or warm up the farm above growth threshold. Whilst hydroponic farms require less maintenance, players should first practice how to cool water to a specific temperature to prevent pipe breakages (e.g. thermal sensors deactivating
Wheezewort
built on doors).
If warm water is used for irrigation the air surrounding the Sleet Wheat needs to be constantly cooled. This can be achieved using
Wheezewort
.
Liquid Valves
, when set slightly below Sleet Wheat's consumption rate, can be used to minimize the amount of water idly sitting in the pipes. Each 3 Sleet Wheat tiles require 100 grams of water per second. (Care should be taken with this setup to maintain the growth rate and evenly distribute water without stumbling over internal buffers.)
Sleet Wheat farms are inherently resistant to spoilage and
Food Poisoning
, since they require such cold temperatures.
Using a
Fertilizer Synthesizer
and
Farm Station
increases the overall dirt efficiency of the farm, although it requires the addition of
Phosphorite
.
The used
Dirt
temperatures affects the plants. To avoid heating up, store them inside a
Storage Bin
submerged in cold
Polluted Water
or similar.
If Frost Bun is seen as the base line amount of Calories, 1200 / 3, then each Sleet Wheat Grain provides 400 kcals.
This means that the net caloric gain of Berry Sludge and Frost Burger is +400 kcals. Pepper Bread and Mixed Berry Pie is +0 kcals.
Sleet Wheat with Frost Bun provide a baseline 400 Calories per day, if the plants drop instead of being harvested it decreases to 327.
Pepper Bread does not increase the effective kcal, meaning it remains 400 and 327 respectively.
Overall, the Sleet Wheat Farm should be either well insulated using
Insulated Tiles
or placed in a
Frozen Biome
.
Database
Sleet Wheat
Edible Plant
The Sleet Wheat plant has become so well-adapted to cold environments, it is no longer able to survive at room temperatures.
The grain of the Sleet Wheat can be ground down into high quality foodstuffs, or planted to cultivate further Sleet Wheat plants.
