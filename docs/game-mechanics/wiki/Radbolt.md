# Radbolt

This article is related to
Spaced Out
content.
Radbolts are radioactive particles produced in
Radbolt Generators
and
Manual Radbolt Generators
to be used in research and space travel. Once emitted into the world, radbolt projectiles follow a straight line where they can be redirected by
Radbolt Reflectors
and
Radbolt Joint Plates
while constantly decaying into low levels of
radiation
and
Radioactive Contaminants
.
Radbolt projectiles can impact on
Duplicants
,
Critters
, other radbolts and solid materials, possibly releasing their energy in a brief
radiation
explosion along with some
Nuclear Fallout
and heat. They are made of
1 kg
of
Genetic Ooze
.
Radbolt Chambers
can store up to 1000 radbolts while preventing their decay.
Production
Manual Radbolt Generators
convert
Uranium Ore
or
Enriched Uranium
into radbolts and
Depleted Uranium
.
1 kg Uranium Ore = 5 radbolts + 500 g Depleted Uranium.
1 kg Enriched Uranium = 25 radbolts + 800 g Depleted Uranium.
Radbolt Generators
collect 1 radbolt per cycle for each 10 rads per cycle of ambiant
radiation
.
Usage
Materials Study Terminals
will convert 10 radbolts into 1
Applied Sciences Research
point.
Diamond Presses
can turn radbolts into
Diamond
. 1000 radbolts + 100 kg
Refined Carbon
= 100 kg Diamond.
Radbolt Engines
consume 200 radbolts per hex traveled.
Interplanetary Launchers
consume 10 radbolts per hex distance per payload.
Radbolts in motion
464 Radbolts only emitting 2 rads per cycle
While in motion, radbolt projectiles lose 0.1 radbolt per tile, killing any germs and leaving behind 5
Radioactive Contaminant
'germs' in that medium. In a vacuum, projectiles still lose radbolts but don't leave behind contaminants.
Radbolt projectiles always emit
0.2 rads/cycle (Mostly Safe)
in a radius of 3 tiles.
If a projectile runs out of radbolts, it simply vanishes without releasing anything.
Radbolt collisions
When projectiles collide with
Duplicants
,
Critters
, other radbolts, or a tile (including
Airflow Tiles
and
Mesh Tiles
, but not
Pneumatic Doors
or open
Mechanized Airlocks
), the following is released on the impact tile
per radbolt contained
:
33.3 rads per cycle of radiation for 1 second, in a radius of 6 tiles
1 g of
Nuclear Fallout
emitted at
4726.85 °C
/
8540.33 °F
for every radbolt contained in the projectile
50
Radioactive Contaminants
Collisions with duplicants and critters will inflict them with 20
damage points
while duplicants also get applied with 50 Radioactive Contaminants per radbolt as surface germs.
Note if there is no space available for
Nuclear Fallout
to spawn in (which is commonplace for solid tile collisions with an atmosphere), mutual mass deletion occurs and the
Nuclear Fallout
is deleted. For collisions with natural tiles, the mass is reduced by the amount of
Nuclear Fallout
deleted. For constructed solid tiles, its internal mass is reduced which results in the tile forming "ghost tiles" once all mass is removed. Radbolts are then free to pass through ghost tiles. Unlike natural tiles, the internal mass is reset to its original mass on reload.
Since the explosion is so brief, even 500 radbolts only result in a low amount of radiation absorbed by a nearby Duplicant (500 radbolts = 16666.7 rads per cycle = 27.8 rads absorbed, ignoring the effects of the nuclear fallout).

Tips
Since radbolt projectiles are harmful to critters, they can be used as a means to automate
Meat
production without waiting for the critter to starve or drown.
